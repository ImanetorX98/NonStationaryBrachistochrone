\title{Constrained-worldline brachistochrones in non-stationary
spacetimes: Kodama rails, closed forms, and plunge inversion}

\author{Iman Rosignoli}
\affiliation{University of Pavia, Pavia, Italy\\
\normalfont\texttt{iman.rosignoli01@universitadipavia.it}}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We extend the relativistic brachistochrone (fastest-descent constrained rail
curves, Perlick 1991) from stationary to non-stationary spacetimes. The rail
constraint $-u\cdot W=\Ehat$ is an actively controlled invariant, legitimate via
Pontryagin's principle, with the selector $W$ following a hierarchy Killing $\to$
conformal Killing $\to$ Kodama vector; we treat FLRW, Vaidya
($W=\partial_v$, the Kodama vector), and Thakurta--Kerr. Our main results are:
Kodama energy conservation along the rail; a plunge-radius law and a branch-intrinsic
depth ordering (rotation and the conformal factor invert the plunge under a
same-launch comparison, with no evaporative inversion at the frozen-clock level);
closed-form equatorial separatrices in Weierstrass functions, continued through the
ergosphere, with a trichotomy and an analytic cusp theorem; an existence theorem for
the conformal plunge inversion; and first-order adiabatic corrections whose
\emph{on-shell} component reduces in closed form to length-two iterated Abelian
integrals on the frozen spectral curve, unified across the three geometries by a
breathing-indicatrix structure (the weight-two letter migrating from a third-kind
dipole at infinity to a $\mathrm{d}\!\log$ at the horizon). The \emph{off-shell}
costate piece, of the same class, is derived from the extended Hamiltonian and
verified against the true non-autonomous flow.
\emph{Status.} The controlled-rail formulation, the closed-form separatrices and cusp
theorem, and the on-shell first-order reduction are proved. The fixed-endpoint
no-inversion is proved for $r_0\le R_\ast(E)$ (and, statically, as $r_0\to\infty$),
machine-certified at representative $r_0$, conjectural in general. Interior-ergosphere
optimality, the outgoing-Vaidya boundary-value problem, an explicit off-shell closed
form, and a rigorous higher-genus polylogarithm framework remain open. Block
coefficients are obtained in explicit symbolic form; the iterated Abelian integrals
are evaluated numerically by a geometrically convergent theta-nome ($q$-)series
supplied in the reproducibility package (their irreducibility and minimal basis
dimension stated as conjectures). Every symbolic identity is independently confirmed
in Mathematica.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
The relativistic brachistochrone---the fastest constrained ``rail'' worldline
between two events---was formulated by Perlick for stationary spacetimes
\cite{Perlick1991,PerlickRayOptics}, where a timelike Killing vector provides
the conserved rail energy. It connects naturally to Fermat's principle and to
optical (Randers--Zermelo--Finsler) geometry
\cite{Kovner1990,Randers1941,GibbonsWerner2008,GibbonsRanders2009,BaoRoblesShen}.
Building on our earlier relativistic-brachistochrone studies---the stationary
Kerr geometry \cite{RosignoliKerr}, the interior Schwarzschild metric
\cite{RosignoliIntSchw}, and Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff stellar interiors
\cite{RosignoliTOV}---we extend the construction to \emph{non-stationary}
spacetimes: FLRW, Vaidya \cite{Vaidya1951}, and Thakurta--Kerr
\cite{Thakurta1981}.
These extensions raise three questions that organize the paper.
\emph{(i) Is the rail constraint legitimate without a Killing vector?} We show it
is: $-u\cdot W=\Ehat$ is an actively controlled invariant, and the fastest
constrained worldline solves a well-posed time-optimal control problem
(Sec.~\ref{sec:principle}) whose control cost measures precisely the missing
symmetry. \emph{(ii) What replaces the conserved energy?} In a spherically
symmetric dynamical spacetime the selector $W$ is the Kodama vector, whose flux
charge is the Misner--Sharp mass; the rail conserves the Kodama energy
identically even though it is \emph{not} Noether-conserved for free fall
(Sec.~\ref{sec:vaidya}). \emph{(iii) Which phenomena are genuinely
non-stationary?} We isolate three: capture by cosmological expansion, the
endpoint-sensitive (future-anticipating) turning point in Vaidya, and the breathing
freezing surface that eats the radius of convergence of the quasi-constants in
Thakurta--Kerr. The three spacetimes form a ladder of increasing structure---%
FLRW (degenerate base), Vaidya (dynamical horizon), Thakurta--Kerr
(rotation $+$ conformal expansion)---treated in
Secs.~\ref{sec:flrw}--\ref{sec:thakurta}; equatorial closed forms and the
plunge-inversion analysis follow in Secs.~\ref{sec:closed}--\ref{sec:bvp}.
Table~\ref{tab:claims} states each principal claim with its status (proved /
machine-certified / verified / conjectural) and supporting evidence; the adiabatic
first-order result is deliberately split into three separate claims (on-shell closed
form; exact off-shell quadrature; explicit off-shell special-function assembly, open).

\section{The controlled-rail principle}
\label{sec:principle}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Rail constraint and the indicatrix}
\label{sec:indicatrix}
The rail is a constrained worldline with the invariant
\begin{equation}
    -\,u\cdot W \;=\; \Ehat ,
    \label{eq:rail}
\end{equation}
actively maintained by an ideal rail force $f_\mu=Du_\mu/\dd\tau$ with
$f\cdot u=0$ (automatic for a rest-mass--preserving force), which creates no rest
mass but is free to exert power against the selector.

\paragraph{The ellipse.} Fix an event and a time function adapted to $W$. The
linear constraint~\eqref{eq:rail} fixes the $W$-component of $u$; the mass shell
$u\cdot u=-1$ is a quadric; their intersection, projected onto the physical
velocity plane $(\dd r,\,r\,\dd\varphi)$ per unit selector-time, is an ellipse
\begin{equation}
    \dot x(\theta)=c+R(\theta),\qquad \theta\in[0,2\pi),
    \label{eq:ellipse}
\end{equation}
whose center $c$ is the Zermelo wind (zero for FLRW, ingoing radial for Vaidya,
azimuthal---frame dragging---for Thakurta--Kerr) and whose axes $R$ shrink with
the local speed as the freezing surface is approached (Fig.~\ref{fig:indicatrici}).
Every branch---arrival time $t$, proper time $\tau$, conformal time $\eta$---shares
this \emph{same} indicatrix; the branches differ only in the cost one-form, i.e.\
in which clock measures the elapsed travel time.

\emph{Domain.} The oval~\eqref{eq:ellipse} is compact and strictly convex---the
hypothesis of the Pontryagin reduction---precisely where $W$ is \emph{timelike} and
the intersection is nondegenerate. Three loci need separate treatment: the freezing
surface, where the oval collapses ($R\to0$, the turning-point/separatrix locus); the
horizon, where the radial coefficient degenerates; and, for Thakurta--Kerr, the
\emph{ergosphere}, where $\partial_\eta$ (hence $W$) is spacelike, so
$-u\cdot W=\Ehat$ fixes a spatial velocity component and the mass-shell intersection
is no longer automatically a PMP oval. The local theorems below hold in the
timelike-$W$, nondegenerate domain; the ergosphere is reached by analytic
continuation (Sec.~\ref{sec:trichotomy}); we do \emph{not} claim proved optimality
inside the ergosphere. Re-anchoring the constraint to a timelike (Doran/ZAMO)
congruence is a genuinely different, inequivalent construction (it changes $W$, the
indicatrix, the cost and the Hamiltonian) and is left to future work; a coordinate
change alone does not alter the geometric selector.

\paragraph{Pontryagin reduction.} Minimizing travel time subject
to~\eqref{eq:ellipse} is a time-optimal control problem with control $\theta$.
The indicatrix~\eqref{eq:ellipse} is a compact, strictly convex closed \emph{curve},
and the control is its direction $\theta\in S^1$; strict convexity makes the
support-function maximizer unique, so no Filippov relaxation is needed (the control
set $S^1$ is compact)---we do \emph{not} claim the velocity locus is a convex
\emph{subset} of the plane, nor convexify it. Excluding $p=0$ (nontriviality), the
optimal control is single-valued and smooth and Pontryagin's maximum principle
\cite{Pontryagin,Liberzon} delivers $H(x,p)=\max_\theta p\cdot\dot x(\theta)$ in
closed form (Secs.~\ref{sec:vaidya}--\ref{sec:thakurta}). Being a genuine
time-optimal control problem, the correct prescription is the Pontryagin
(vakonomic-type) one; we do not invoke the nonholonomic d'Alembert principle, whose
equations differ unless the constraint distribution is integrable---which convexity
does not entail. Two structural facts hold throughout: $p_\varphi=J$ is conserved (axial symmetry),
and free-arrival-time transversality fixes the branch Hamiltonian to vanish
\emph{at the free endpoint},
\begin{equation}
    H\big|_{s=s_f}=0 \qquad\text{(free arrival time).}
    \label{eq:transversality}
\end{equation}
For a non-autonomous system $H$ is \emph{not} a global first integral: along the
extremal $\dd H/\dd s=\partial_s H\propto m'(v)$ (Vaidya) or $A'(\eta)$
(Thakurta--Kerr), so $H\neq0$ in the interior (consistently, $p_v(r_1)=0$ at the
arrival radius but $p_v(r)\neq0$ along the path). The globally conserved object is
the extended Hamiltonian $\mathcal H=p_s+H=0$ ($p_s$ conjugate to $s$); only in the
stationary limit is $H$ autonomous and $H\equiv0$ a genuine first integral.

\paragraph{Cost of control.} For an accelerated worldline with rail four-force
$a^\mu=Du^\mu/\dd\tau$, the invariant obeys the \emph{exact} identity
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\dd(-u\cdot W)}{\dd\tau}=-\,a\cdot W-u^a u^b\,\nabla_{(a}W_{b)}.
    \label{eq:cost}
\end{equation}
The constraint $a\cdot u=0$ is \emph{automatic} for a rest-mass--preserving four-force
(not a special ``magnetic'' property, and not by itself zero work relative to an
observer): it means only that no rest mass is created, while the rail exerts power
\emph{against} $W$. A geodesic
($a=0$) lets $-u\cdot W$ drift at $-u^au^b\nabla_{(a}W_{b)}$, zero for all $u$ iff
$W$ is Killing; to hold $-u\cdot W=\Ehat$ fixed the rail supplies exactly
$a\cdot W=-u^au^b\nabla_{(a}W_{b)}$, so~\eqref{eq:cost} vanishes. This control power
is the failure of the Killing equation---the missing symmetry: $\Ehat$ is actively
controlled, not conserved. The optical reductions of $H$
reproduce the Randers/Zermelo structure
\cite{GibbonsRanders2009,CaponioWind}.

\paragraph{Thrust and fuel.} As $a^\mu=Du^\mu/\dd\tau$ is the \emph{proper}
acceleration (gravity is in the covariant derivative, so a geodesic reads $a=0$),
the rail force is the thrust per unit mass a rocket must supply; $|a|=\sqrt{a\cdot a}$
integrates to $\Delta v=\int|a|\dd\tau$ and, \emph{heuristically} under a chosen
constant-exhaust model (not needed for the mathematical argument), to a fuel fraction
$1-e^{-\Delta v/v_{\rm e}}$. A Schwarzschild arc ($E=1.4$, $r:10M\!\to\!5.7M$) gives
$\Delta v\approx0.6c$ (a photon rocket burns $\sim45\%$ of its mass); $|a|$ diverges
toward the freezing/horizon surface, so the fuel diverges there
(\texttt{brachistochrone\_thrust\_fuel.py}). The brachistochrone is the fastest rail
curve, not the fuel-minimal one (an unpowered free-fall geodesic needs no rail thrust):
this is the price of
speed at fixed $\Ehat$, the $a\cdot W$ term adding the thrust that fights the
non-stationary drift.

\paragraph{Geometric dictionary.} The indicatrix~\eqref{eq:ellipse} carries two
independent data---its \emph{center} $c=(c_r,c_\varphi)$ (the Zermelo wind) and
the \emph{eccentricity} of its semi-axes (the anisotropy of the local speed,
$a_r\neq a_\varphi$)---and each governs a distinct feature of the
brachistochrones, the same for every branch:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item eccentricity $\neq0$ (a spatial gradient $\partial_r f\neq0$)
$\Rightarrow$ the $t$ and $\tau$ branches split in depth, $n_t/n_\tau=E/f\neq1$;
\item azimuthal offset $c_\varphi\neq0$ (frame dragging)
$\Rightarrow$ rotational plunge inversion becomes possible;
\item radial offset $c_r\neq0$ (a non-stationary wind, $m'\neq0$)
$\Rightarrow$ endpoint-sensitive memory, $p_v\neq0$ along the path;
\item shrinking axes $R\to0$ (local speed $v\to0$) $\Rightarrow$ freezing and
capture.
\end{itemize}
Eccentricity and center offset are \emph{separate} levers: the first switches on
the $t/\tau$ split, the second the inversions. In particular the three branches
\emph{degenerate}---the $t$, $\tau$ and $\eta$ brachistochrones coincide---iff the
indicatrix is, up to a position-independent conformal scale, a rigid centered
circle ($c=0$, $a_r=a_\varphi$, radius depending on the flow parameter alone),
equivalently iff the optical cost ratios $n_t/n_\tau,\,n_t/n_\eta$ are
position-independent. This is exactly spatial homogeneity: FLRW is the unique
such case in our ladder (Sec.~\ref{sec:flrw}), whereas any gradient or wind
breaks it---even the static, non-rotating Schwarzschild ellipse, which is
centered ($c=0$) yet eccentric, already splits $t$ from $\tau$. The indicatrix
shape is thus the organizing thread of the paper: FLRW (circle) $\to$ Vaidya
(radial wind) $\to$ Thakurta--Kerr (angular wind) is a progression of ellipse
deformations, each switching on one further phenomenon.

\subsection{Selecting $W$: the Killing--CKV--Kodama hierarchy}
\label{sec:whierarchy}
The selector $W$ is fixed by a hierarchy of decreasing symmetry, each level
recovering the previous one in the appropriate limit.

\emph{(a) Killing.} If a timelike Killing vector $\xi$ exists, $W=\xi$ and the
right-hand side of Eq.~\eqref{eq:cost} vanishes identically: $-u\cdot\xi$ is
Noether-conserved and the rail is free. This is Perlick's stationary case
\cite{Perlick1991}.

\emph{(b) Conformal Killing.} If the spacetime is conformally stationary,
$g=\Omega^2\hat g$ with $\hat g$ stationary, the timelike conformal Killing
vector $\partial_\eta$ selects the conformal-time branch; $-u\cdot W$ is exactly
conserved for null rays and controlled up to the conformal factor for massive
rails. FLRW and constant-$A$ Thakurta--Kerr live at this level.

\emph{(c) Kodama.} In a spherically symmetric \emph{dynamical} spacetime with no
Killing or conformal Killing vector, the Kodama vector
$K^a=\varepsilon^{ab}\nabla_b r$ \cite{Kodama1980,Hayward1996} is the canonical
replacement: it is divergence-free (Eq.~\eqref{eq:kodama}), reduces to $\xi$ in
the static limit, and its flux $G^{ab}K_b$ is conserved with charge the
Misner--Sharp mass. Vaidya is the paradigm (Sec.~\ref{sec:kodama-energy}).

\emph{(d) Convention.} Absent even spherical symmetry the Kodama construction
fails and $W$ becomes a declared convention, with no distinguished normalization
of the cost one-form. Levels (a)--(c) are what make the non-stationary rail
canonical rather than arbitrary.

\section{FLRW: the degenerate base case}
\label{sec:flrw}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
For spatially flat FLRW, $g=a(\eta)^2\eta_{\rm Mink}$, the rail uses the
conformal Killing vector $\partial_\eta$; the local speed is
\begin{equation}
    v(\eta)=\sqrt{1-a(\eta)^2/\Ehat^{2}},
    \label{eq:flrwspeed}
\end{equation}
vanishing at the freezing barrier $a=\Ehat$.
\paragraph{Branch degeneracy.} Because $a=a(\eta)$ depends on conformal time
alone (spatial homogeneity), the optical index built from the conformal Killing
vector,
\begin{equation}
    n(\eta,r)=\frac1v=\frac{\Ehat}{\sqrt{\Ehat^2-a(\eta)^2}},
    \label{eq:flrwindex}
\end{equation}
is \emph{position-independent} at fixed $\eta$. The conformal-time functional
$T_\eta=\int n\,\dd\ell_{\rm flat}$ is then minimized by a flat straight line
(Fermat with a purely time-dependent index). The same curve minimizes $T_t$ and
$T_\tau$: with $\dd t=a\,\dd\eta$ and $\dd\tau=a\sqrt{1-\dot{\vec x}^2}\,\dd\eta$,
the monotone reparametrization $t(\eta)=\int a\,\dd\eta$ maps critical points to
critical points, and the $\tau$-cost differs from the $\eta$-cost only by the
same $\eta$-dependent weight. Hence
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
    \arg\min T_t&=\arg\min T_\tau=\arg\min T_\eta\\
    &=\text{comoving straight line}.
\end{split}
    \label{eq:flrwdegen}
\end{equation}
The $t/\tau$ splitting that drives every later result is a gravitational-%
\emph{gradient} effect ($\partial_r f\neq0$); it is absent whenever the metric
functions depend on time only. FLRW is thus the degenerate base of the ladder:
it exhibits freezing at $a=\Ehat$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:flrw-kin})
but no branch structure (Fig.~\ref{fig:flrw-var}).

\section{Vaidya: dynamical black hole and the Kodama rail}
\label{sec:vaidya}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
The ingoing Vaidya metric $\dd s^2=-f\,\dd v^2+2\,\dd v\,\dd r+r^2\dd\Omega^2$,
$f=1-2m(v)/r$ \cite{Vaidya1951,LindquistSchwartzMisner1965}, has no timelike
Killing vector; its trapping structure is that of a dynamical horizon
\cite{AshtekarKrishnan2003,Booth2005,FaraoniBook}. The Kodama vector
\cite{Kodama1980,Hayward1996,AbreuVisser2010}
\begin{equation}
    K^a=\varepsilon^{ab}\nabla_b r = \partial_v ,\qquad
    \nabla_a K^a=0,
    \label{eq:kodama}
\end{equation}
is divergence-free without any Killing symmetry, and its charge is the
Misner--Sharp mass $m(v)$ \cite{MisnerSharp1964,HaywardMukohyama1999}.

\subsection{Kodama energy is the rail invariant}
\label{sec:kodama-energy}
From the rail constraint alone one finds the exact identity
$-g(T,T)=(f\,u^v-1)^2/\Ehat^2$, hence
\begin{equation}
    -\,u\cdot K \;=\; \Ehat \quad\text{identically along the brachistochrone.}
    \label{eq:kodama-conserved}
\end{equation}
This is \emph{controlled} conservation, not Noether: for a geodesic
$-u\cdot K$ would drift at rate $-m'(u^v)^2/r\propto m'$, exactly the
right-hand side of Eq.~\eqref{eq:cost} for $W=K$. Only the unnormalized
$K=\partial_v$ has zero control cost in the static limit; the normalized
$\hat K=K/\sqrt f$ does not (discriminating test), which is why the Kodama
vector, not a unit selector, is the canonical choice.

\paragraph{Indicatrix and Hamiltonians.} With $w\equiv\Ehat^2-f$ the constraint
$-u_v=\Ehat$ turns the admissible velocities at fixed $(v,r)$ into an ellipse,
\begin{equation}
    r'=(f-\Ehat^2)+\Ehat\sqrt w\,\cos\theta,\qquad
    \varphi'=\frac{\sqrt w}{r}\sin\theta,
    \label{eq:vaidya-ind}
\end{equation}
a Zermelo problem with ingoing radial wind $f-\Ehat^2<0$. Maximizing
$p\cdot\dot x$ over $\theta$ at fixed $p_\varphi=J$ gives the closed-form branch
Hamiltonians
\begin{align}
    H_v&=p_r(f-\Ehat^2)-1+\sqrt w\,\sqrt{\Ehat^2 p_r^2+J^2/r^2},
    \label{eq:Hv}\\
    H_\tau&=p_r(f-\Ehat^2)-\Ehat
        +\sqrt w\,\sqrt{(\Ehat p_r+1)^2+J^2/r^2}.
    \label{eq:Htau-vaidya}
\end{align}
The $\tau$-branch is the $v$-branch under $p_r\to p_r+1/\Ehat$ with unit cost
replaced by $\Ehat$. Non-stationarity is the explicit dependence
$\dd H/\dd v=\partial_v H\propto m'(v)$; the \emph{teleological}
(here: \emph{endpoint-sensitive}, from the free-future two-point problem---not
causal) momentum $p_v=-H$
satisfies $p_v(r_1)=0$ at the free arrival radius yet $p_v(r)\neq0$ along the
path, so the turning point \emph{anticipates future accretion}
(Fig.~\ref{fig:vaidya-traj}).
The conserved $J=p_\varphi$ is the Noether \emph{costate} of the control Hamiltonian,
not a priori the mechanical $g(u,\partial_\varphi)$; the two coincide here because
$\partial_\varphi$ is Killing for both metric and control, but the Legendre map is
\emph{branch-specific} (native $v/t$ value $J$, proper-time $\tilde p_\varphi=J-A^2b/\Ehat$),
and the conformal weight makes $J_{\rm eff}=J/A=g^{\rm Kerr}(u^{\rm Kerr},\partial_\varphi)$
the Kerr-frame unit-mass charge. We reserve ``angular momentum'' for $g(u,\partial_\varphi)$
and call $J$ the branch costate where it matters.

\paragraph{Sanity check: rail energy in Thakurta--Kerr.} The same test applies
to Thakurta--Kerr, where---the geometry being only axisymmetric---no Kodama
vector exists and the rail invariant is tied instead to the conformal Killing
vector $\partial_\eta$ (factor $A'/A$). On a \emph{geodesic} the energy
$\Ehat=-g^{\rm TK}(u,\partial_\eta)$ drifts at exactly $\mathrm{d}\Ehat/\mathrm{d}\tau=A'/A$
(numerically to $\sim\!10^{-11}$); on the \emph{brachistochrone}---instantaneously
an on-shell Kerr orbit at $E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A(\eta)$---the rail holds $\Ehat$
constant to $8.9\times10^{-16}$, the exact analogue of the Vaidya value
(\texttt{ThakurtaMetric/rail\_conservation.py}). Hence $E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$
(and, by the same conformal weight, $J_{\rm eff}=J/A$) track along the orbit.
Conservation of $\Ehat$ determines how the parameters \emph{drift}; it does not force
the trajectory to stay on the instantaneous $H=0$ shell (the costate equation is
independent). The frozen relation
$\mathrm{d}\varphi/\mathrm{d}r=F(r;E_{\rm eff},J_{\rm eff})$ and the drift correction
$\delta\varphi=-\int(\Ehat\,\partial_E F+J\,\partial_J F)\,\eta\,\mathrm{d}r$ are thus
the \emph{on-shell component}; the complete first-order term is
Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}, which adds the off-shell (costate $p_\eta$) piece.


\subsection{Phenomenology: penetration, timing, bounce}
\label{sec:vaidya-pheno}
Three features distinguish the dynamical hole from its frozen-mass
Schwarzschild limit.

\emph{Penetration.} The $\tau$-brachistochrone reaches the apparent horizon
$r=2m(v)$ only if $|J|$ lies below a threshold $J_c(v_0)$; accretion ($m'>0$)
opens a \emph{finite} window $J_c>0$, whereas evaporation ($m'<0$) closes it to
zero measure---an accretion/evaporation asymmetry with no static analogue. The
finite capture interval $J\in(0,\Jc]$ is the analogue of the Kerr $t$-branch
dichotomy, generated here by the \emph{dynamics}: grazing orbits linger near
periapsis while the growing horizon closes the gap.

\emph{Bounce.} The $r$-parametrization degenerates at periapsis
($\dd v/\dd r\to\infty$); reparametrizing by $v$ makes the Euler--Lagrange
problem regular at the turning point $r'=0$, so the trajectory is integrated
straight \emph{through} the bounce---the same extremal, via the exact change of
variable $(f v'-1)\dd r=(f-r')\dd v$. The $v$-flow reaches the periapsis exactly
where the $r$-flow diverges ($r_{\min}=3.0105$ at $v=7.29$ for $\mu\equiv m'=0.01$),
and the endpoint-sensitive memory $p_v(r)\propto m'$ measures the departure from the
frozen-mass Schwarzschild curve (Fig.~\ref{fig:vaidya-bounce}).

\emph{Timing and the orbit--horizon race.} Because $m$ grows along the path,
$r_{\min}$ depends on \emph{when} the particle arrives, the epoch $v_1$---a
genuinely non-stationary effect absent in Kerr. Strikingly, the absolute and
relative penetration have \emph{opposite} winners
(Fig.~\ref{fig:vaidya-timing}): under evaporation the absolute periapsis descends
but the horizon $2m(v)$ retreats faster, so the relative penetration
$r_{\min}/2m(v_{\rm peri})$ \emph{worsens} for later arrivals; under accretion the
periapsis rises yet the horizon grows faster, so late arrivals graze the relative
horizon. The orbit--horizon chase has reversed outcomes measured in $r$ versus in
horizon units.

\subsection{Plunge-radius law and the (absent) evaporative inversion}
\label{sec:vaidya-plunge}
The turning-point law has the static Schwarzschild form evaluated at the mass
at periapsis, $f_*=1-2m(v_{\rm peri})/r_{\min}$:
\begin{align}
    \tau:&\quad (\Ehat^2-f_*)J^2 = f_* r_{\min}^2 ,\\
    t:&\quad (\Ehat^2-f_*)J^2 = \Ehat^2 r_{\min}^2/f_* ,
    \label{eq:vaidya-turn}
\end{align}
plus a genuine $O(m')$ correction from the endpoint-sensitive costate memory $p_v$. The
static ratio $J^2_t/J^2_\tau=\Ehat^2/f^2>1$ gives $r_{\min}^t<r_{\min}^\tau$
(``$t$ deeper''), a \emph{branch-intrinsic} ordering set by $\Ehat/f>1$ alone,
independent of the sign of $m'$ and of the ingoing/outgoing flux. Physical
evaporation is the \emph{outgoing} Vaidya metric $\dd s^2=-f\dd u^2-2\dd u\,\dd r$
(retarded $u$), not the ingoing metric with $m'<0$. Because the ordering is
branch-intrinsic it is, to this static/frozen-clock order, unchanged under evaporation.
The apparent linear-model ``inversion'' from flipping $m'<0$ in the ingoing model is an
unphysical extrapolation to $m\le0$. We do \emph{not} here solve the full outgoing
optimal-control problem (its own Hamiltonians, costates and transversality): the
accretion--evaporation asymmetry [Eq.~\eqref{eq:vaidya-asymmetry}] is a clock
decomposition ($v\!\leftrightarrow\!u$, tortoise sign) of the frozen first-order
correction, not an outgoing boundary-value solution; we thus state the absence of an
evaporative inversion at the branch-intrinsic static level and leave the full outgoing
BVP to future work.

\subsection{Closed-form adiabatic correction and the horizon dilogarithm}
\label{sec:vaidya-adiabatic}
For slowly varying $m(v)$ the frozen-coefficient WKB scheme of
Sec.~\ref{sec:adiabatic} applies with $m(v)$ in place of the conformal factor and
$\dot m=\dd m/\dd v$ the small parameter. The frozen family is the Schwarzschild
brachistochrone---the $a\to0$ limit of the Thakurta--Kerr forms---on
$S=r(r-2m)DE\,[r^2(r-2m)-J^2 DE]$, $DE=(E^2-1)r+2m$, which keeps six distinct roots:
the constrained brachistochrone is genus \emph{two} even in Schwarzschild (only
geodesics are elliptic). The mass derivative reduces exactly (horizon factor
cancelling), $\partial_m F=N_m/S^{3/2}$,
$\partial_m\varphi_0=\mathcal{A}^m/\sqrt S+\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k^m U_k$, $U_k=\int
r^k/\sqrt S\,\dd r$, algebraic $c_k^m$ (verified to $10^{-15}$). The clock is the
advanced time: from $v=t+r_*$,
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\dd v}{\dd r}=\frac{E\,r^3}{\sqrt S}+\frac{r}{r-2m}
    \;\Rightarrow\; v=E\,U_3+r+2m\ln(r-2m),
    \label{eq:vaidya-clock}
\end{equation}
the tortoise term injecting a horizon logarithm. This is the advanced-time (native)
branch; the \emph{proper-time} branch uses the second-kind clock
$\dd\tau/\dd r=r^2(r-2m)/\sqrt S$ ($\eta=U_3-2m U_2$, $b=(0,0,-2m,1,0)$, no horizon
log) and closes by the same assembly~\eqref{eq:psi-split} with $Q_{kj}=c_k^m b_j-c_j^m
b_k$, all coefficients algebraic (verified to $10^{-14}$). The advanced-time
first-order correction integrates by parts into a fully explicit closed form (verified
to $9\times10^{-14}$),
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\delta\varphi}{\dot m}=A_m\,v-E\!\int\! A_m\frac{r^3}{\sqrt S}\dd r
    -\int\! A_m\frac{r}{r-2m}\dd r,\quad A_m\equiv\partial_m\varphi_0,
    \label{eq:vaidya-deltaphi}
\end{equation}
This is the \emph{on-shell} component (mass-drift of the frozen family). As in
Thakurta--Kerr the interior costate does not vanish---$p_v=-H_v=O(\dot m)$ with
$p_v(r_1)=0$ only at the free arrival---so the complete first-order term adds the
off-shell piece given by the Vaidya analogue of Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}
($\Theta=m\partial_m$, terminally anchored $S$), a length-two iterated integral of the
same horizon-dilogarithm class; the identical canonical perturbation theory applied to
$H_v$ closes the Vaidya first order to $O(\dot m^2)$ (residual slope $\approx2$;
\texttt{VaidyaMetric/vaidya\_first\_order\_offshell.py}).
Writing $A_m=\mathcal{A}^m/\sqrt S-\alpha_0+\sum_k c_k^m U_k$ with the constant
$\alpha_0=\mathcal{A}^m(r_0)/\sqrt{S(r_0)}$, integrating by parts in each factor
gives the \emph{fully assembled} closed form (verified to $2\times10^{-13}$)
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \frac{\delta\varphi}{\dot m}
    ={}&A_m\,v
    -E\Big[\mathcal{I}_{\rm poly}-\alpha_0 U_3
        +\tfrac12\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k^m\big(U_kU_3-W_{3k}\big)\Big]\\
    &{}-\Big[\mathcal{L}_{2m}-\alpha_0(v-E\,U_3)\\
    &\qquad\quad{}+\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k^m\big(r\,U_k-U_{k+1}+2m\,\mathcal{D}_k\big)\Big],
\end{aligned}
    \label{eq:vaidya-full}
\end{equation}
expanding into: the abelian integrals $U_k=\int r^k/\sqrt S\,\dd r$; the elementary
rational-log primitive $\mathcal{I}_{\rm poly}=\int\mathcal{A}^m r^3/S\,\dd r$; the
genus-two polylogarithms $W_{3k}=\int(U_3\dd U_k-U_k\dd U_3)$ at infinity (as in
Kerr); the \emph{second}-kind horizon integral
$\mathcal{L}_{2m}=\int\mathcal{A}^m r/[(r-2m)\sqrt S]\,\dd r$ (as
$\mathcal{A}^m(2m)\ne0$ the integrand is $\sim(r-2m)^{-3/2}$: a double pole with no
residue at the horizon Weierstrass point, closed by the Kleinian
$\zeta$/quasi-periods~\cite{BuchstaberEnolskii1997,BakerAbelian1897}, not Fay); the
genuinely third-kind object is the horizon letter $\dd r/(r-2m)$ (tortoise log, simple
pole residue two), closing as a Fay $\theta$-ratio~\cite{Fay1973} as $L$
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:thirdkind-theta}] but centred at $r=2m$; and
the \emph{horizon dilogarithms} $\mathcal{D}_k=\int U_k/(r-2m)\,\dd r$. Because $r=2m$
is a branch point of $S$, in $t=\sqrt{r-2m}$ one has $\dd r/(r-2m)=2\,\dd t/t$---a
simple pole of residue two, a third-kind $\dd\log$ letter \emph{at the horizon}. By
the residue theorem it cannot stand alone: on the compact genus-two curve
$\dd r/(r-2m)$ has residue $+2$ at the horizon Weierstrass point and $-1$ at each of
the two points at infinity $\infty_\pm$ (sum zero), so its Fay/prime-form
$\theta$-ratio carries the full divisor $2\,(r{=}2m)-\infty_+-\infty_-$. Its
elementary primitive is $\ln(r-2m)$, so one integration by parts closes $\mathcal{D}_k$
onto a single weight-two letter (verified to $10^{-12}$),
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{D}_k=U_k\ln(r-2m)-\mathcal{G}_k,\qquad
    \mathcal{G}_k=\int\ln(r-2m)\,\frac{r^k}{\sqrt S}\,\dd r,
    \label{eq:horizon-dilog}
\end{equation}
$\mathcal{G}_k$ being the horizon-anchored genus-two dilogarithm---the exact
counterpart of the Kerr $W_{jk}$, same hyperelliptic-dilogarithm class (conjecturally
irreducible; theta-divisor obstruction), but sourced by the advanced-time divergence at the
horizon rather than a third-kind dipole at infinity. On the separatrix $|J|=\Jc$ the
sextic gains a double root at $r_d$, the genus drops to one
($\sqrt S=(r-r_d)\sqrt{Q_4}$), the $U_k$ reduce to elliptic integrals (machine
precision vs.\ direct genus-two quadrature), and $\mathcal{G}_k$ collapses to the
tabulated elliptic (Bloch--Wigner) dilogarithm on $E:w^2=Q_4$---the maximally
explicit reusable form. For $m=1,E=1.4$: $\Jc=7.0266$, $r_d=-3.3637$, branch points
$\{-2.083,0,2m,8.727\}$, $k^2=0.6067$, rectangular nome $\tau=0.90597\,i$ (Legendre
$iK'/K$ and Sage lattice agree to fifteen digits); the horizon $r=2m$ is a branch
point of $Q_4$, hence a two-torsion point carrying the elliptic $\dd\log$ letter.
Here $r_d<0$: this locus is the \emph{algebraic} genus-degeneration $J_{\rm deg}$
(a double root of the sextic), not a physical external separatrix---at $E=1.4$ the
Schwarzschild $\tau$-brachistochrone has no double root at $r>2m$; the reduction is
algebraic and valid for any sign of $r_d$, and $J_{\rm deg}$ is distinct from the
dynamical penetration threshold $J_c(v_0)$ (\texttt{separatrix\_classification.py}). With
the Weierstrass $\sigma,\zeta$ of $E$ (base at the Weierstrass point, involution
$z\to-z$) the weight-one integrals close as third-kind elliptic integrals,
$U_0=\rho[\ln\sigma(z-z_d)-\ln\sigma(z+z_d)]+Cz$, residue $\rho=1/\sqrt{Q_4(r_d)}$ and
$C$ algebraic (derived, verified to $10^{-14}$); one further integration turns
$\mathcal{G}_k$ into the elliptic
dilogarithm~\cite{Bloch2000,BeilinsonLevin1994} $\int\ln\sigma\,\zeta\,\dd z$, with
single-valued projection the tabulated Zagier series~\cite{Zagier1990}
$D^E(z)=\sum_{n\ge0}D(q^n\zeta)-\sum_{n\ge1}D(q^n/\zeta)$. All coefficients
are algebraic; the transcendence lives in $W_{jk},\mathcal{G}_k$, so the correction is
reusable once $S$ and the $c_k^m$ are fixed.

\paragraph{Accretion--evaporation asymmetry.} At first order $\delta\varphi$ is linear
in $\dot m$, so $\dot m\to-\dot m$ na\"ively just flips its sign. The physical
asymmetry is subtler: accretion ($\dot m>0$) is \emph{ingoing} Vaidya, clocked by the
advanced time $v=t+r_*$; evaporation ($\dot m<0$) is \emph{outgoing} Vaidya, clocked by
the retarded time $u=t-r_*$. The frozen reduction $\partial_m F$ is identical (it
depends only on $S$); only the clock changes, and $v\leftrightarrow u$ flips the sign
of the tortoise. With $v=E\,U_3+r_*$, $r_*=(r-r_0)+2m\ln\frac{r-2m}{r_0-2m}$,
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \frac{\delta\varphi}{\dot m}\Big|_{\rm accr}&=\mathcal{A}_\infty+\mathcal{B}_{\rm hor},
    \qquad
    \frac{\delta\varphi}{\dot m}\Big|_{\rm evap}=\mathcal{A}_\infty-\mathcal{B}_{\rm hor},\\
    \mathcal{A}_\infty&=\!\int\!\partial_m F\,E U_3\,\dd r,
    \qquad
    \mathcal{B}_{\rm hor}=\!\int\!\partial_m F\,r_*\,\dd r,
\end{aligned}
    \label{eq:vaidya-asymmetry}
\end{equation}
so the net asymmetry at equal $|\dot m|$ is $2|\dot m|\,\mathcal{B}_{\rm hor}$, carried
\emph{entirely by the tortoise}: the infinity-polylogarithm bulk $\mathcal{A}_\infty$
($E U_3$ sector) is common and cancels. The transcendental, direction-sensitive part of
$\mathcal{B}_{\rm hor}$ is the horizon term $2m\!\int\!\partial_m F\ln(r-2m)\dd r$,
which by parts is the horizon dilogarithm $\mathcal{D}_k$ of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:horizon-dilog}. Thus the accretion/evaporation asymmetry is the physical
face of the horizon weight-two piece---just as the ergosphere-penetration window is the
phenomenological face of the same horizon geometry. For $m=1,E=1.4,J=2.5$:
$\mathcal{A}_\infty=8.7276$, $\mathcal{B}_{\rm hor}=6.1127$ (horizon-log dilogarithm
sector $1.5301$), giving $\delta\varphi/\dot m|_{\rm accr}=14.840$ vs.\
$\delta\varphi/\dot m|_{\rm evap}=2.615$ (verified to $10^{-15}$).

\section{Thakurta--Kerr: conformal rotating compact object}
\label{sec:thakurta}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
Thakurta--Kerr is conformal Kerr, $g=A(\eta)^2 g_{\rm Kerr}$
\cite{Thakurta1981,Kerr1963}, one of a family of cosmological geometries
\cite{McVittie1933,SultanaDyer2005,Kaloper2010,NolanMcVittie1998,MelloMaciel2017,FaraoniBook}.
For constant $A$
the transfer theorem maps the problem to Kerr with $E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$,
$J_{\rm eff}=J/A$. We use $\rp$ ($\Delta=0$) and $\re=2M$ as labels for the \emph{seed}
Kerr null surface and conformal-stationary-limit surface: a conformal factor preserves
the null \emph{character} of $\Delta=0$ (so $\rp$ is a conformal Killing horizon
candidate), but for time-dependent $A(\eta)$ this does not fix the apparent/trapping or
event horizon (which need the null expansions, not computed here). With that caveat
$\rp$ is rigid, $\re=2M$ is crossed regularly, and the freezing surface
$A^2 f=\Ehat^2$ breathes.

\paragraph{Indicatrix and Hamiltonians.} Equatorially, write
$\Delta=r^2-2Mr+a^2$, $\bar v^2=1-A^2f/\Ehat^2$, and
$\bar P=P+A^2(2Ma/r)^2/\Ehat^2$ with $P=r^2+a^2+2Ma^2/r$. The rail
$\Ehat=-u_\eta$ makes the indicatrix an ellipse with an \emph{azimuthal} wind
(frame dragging),
\begin{equation}
    \varphi'_0=\frac{2Ma}{r}\frac{\bar v^2}{\bar P},\qquad
    R^2=f\bar v^2+\bar P\varphi_0'^2=\frac{\Delta\,\bar v^2}{\bar P},
    \label{eq:tk-ind}
\end{equation}
the last equality using the Kerr identity $fP+(2Ma/r)^2=\Delta$. The
conformal-time, coordinate-time and proper-time Hamiltonians are
\begin{align}
    H_\eta&=p_\varphi\varphi'_0
        +R\sqrt{(\Delta/r^2)p_r^2+p_\varphi^2/\bar P}-1,
    \label{eq:Heta}\\
    H_t&=p_\varphi\varphi'_0
        +R\sqrt{(\Delta/r^2)p_r^2+p_\varphi^2/\bar P}-A,
    \label{eq:Ht-tk}\\
    H_\tau&=\tilde p_\varphi\varphi'_0
        +R\sqrt{(\Delta/r^2)p_r^2+\tilde p_\varphi^2/\bar P}-\frac{A^2 f}{\Ehat},
    \label{eq:Htau-tk}
\end{align}
with the conformal gravitomagnetic shift
$\tilde p_\varphi=p_\varphi-2MaA^2/(r\Ehat)$. The arrival-time branch $H_t$ differs
from $H_\eta$ only in the unit cost ($-1\to-A$) and reduces to it for $A=1$; it is
the branch compared with $\tau$ in the plunge inversion
(Sec.~\ref{sec:inversion}), where its turning potential
$H_{t0}=H_t|_{p_r=0}$ enters. All three are regular through the
ergosphere ($f=0\Rightarrow R^2=\bar v^2\Delta/\bar P>0$) and degenerate only at
the horizon $\Delta=0$, which is therefore conformally invariant---independent of
$A$ and $\Ehat$. The three branches share this ellipse: $t$ follows the same
curves as $\eta$ through $t=\int A\,\dd\eta$, while $\tau$ is Riemannian-pure, the
wind cancelling the gravitomagnetic term, so it survives the expansion
(Fig.~\ref{fig:th-branches}).

\paragraph{Non-autonomous optical reduction.} Solving the indicatrix for
$\dd\eta$ at fixed spatial displacement casts the $\eta$-branch as a closed-form
Randers metric, non-autonomous through the conformal factor,
\begin{align}
    \dd\eta &= n(\eta,r)\,\alpha_K+\beta_K,\qquad
    n=\frac{\Ehat}{\sqrt{\Ehat^2-A^2 f}},
    \label{eq:randers}\\
    \alpha_K^2 &= \frac{r^2\,\dd r^2}{f\Delta}+\frac{\Delta\,\dd\varphi^2}{f^2},
    \qquad
    \beta_K = -\frac{2Ma}{rf}\,\dd\varphi.
    \label{eq:randers-data}
\end{align}
The Riemannian data $\alpha_K$ and the one-form $\beta_K$ are \emph{exactly} those
of the \emph{null} Fermat (Randers) metric of Kerr \cite{GibbonsWerner2008}: all
of the mass, rail, and expansion physics is carried by the scalar index $n$,
which multiplies only the Riemannian part, while the frame-dragging one-form
$\beta_K$ is rigid---conformally invariant and independent of $\Ehat$. The
reduction is thus a Zermelo problem with a time-dependent landscape in which only
the metric term breathes, not the wind. It degenerates at the ergosphere ($f=0$,
where $1/F$ is intrinsic, as for stationary Kerr) even though the
Hamiltonian~\eqref{eq:Heta} remains regular there; the limits $\Ehat\to\infty$
(null Kerr optics), $A=1$ (stationary Perlick on Kerr), $a=0$
(Thakurta--Schwarzschild) and $M=a=0$ ($n=1/v$, FLRW) are all recovered.

Fermat data $\alpha$ and wind one-form $\beta$ shared by the branches of a given
metric and only the scalar index $n$ set by the clock. Three structural facts
hold throughout and are verified to machine precision: (i) the proper-time branch
is always \emph{Riemannian-pure}, $\beta_\tau=0$---the wind (ingoing for Vaidya,
gravitomagnetic for Thakurta--Kerr) cancels in $\tau$; (ii) the arrival/native

(straight) geodesic. The $a=0$ limit of the Thakurta $\tau$-row reproduces the
static Vaidya $\tau$-row, and $M=a=0$ (or $m=0$) gives $n=1/v$, FLRW.

\subsection{Quasi-constants and drift}
\label{sec:quasiconst}
For constant $A$ the transfer theorem is exact at the level of conserved
quantities: $u_{\rm TK}=u_{\rm K}/A$ maps the $\Ehat$-rail onto the Kerr rail with
$E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$, so every Kerr quasi-constant carries over by substitution,
\begin{equation}
    K^{\rm TK}=K^{\rm Kerr}(E_{\rm eff}),\qquad
    K_\tau=Q_{\rm std}+a^2 f_2\,p_\theta^2+O(a^4),
    \label{eq:Ktransfer}
\end{equation}
with $Q_{\rm std}$ the Carter constant and $f_2$ the next-to-leading polar
coefficient \cite{Carter1968,RosignoliKerr}. The polar libration that carries the
quasi-constant lives off the equatorial plane, governed by the full Hamiltonian
on a generic slice $\Sigma=r^2+a^2\cos^2\theta$,
\begin{equation}
    H_\tau=\tilde p_\varphi\varphi'_0
      +R\sqrt{\frac{\Delta}{\Sigma}p_r^2+\frac{p_\theta^2}{\Sigma}
      +\frac{\tilde p_\varphi^2}{\bar G}}-\frac{A^2 f_\Sigma}{\Ehat},
    \label{eq:H3d}
\end{equation}
with $f_\Sigma=1-2Mr/\Sigma$, $b=2Mar\sin^2\theta/\Sigma$,
$\bar G=G+A^2 b^2/\Ehat^2$, $\tilde p_\varphi=J-A^2 b/\Ehat$,
$\varphi'_0=b\bar v_\Sigma^2/\bar G$, $R^2=\bar v_\Sigma^2\Delta\sin^2\theta/\bar G$,
and the block identity $f_\Sigma G+b^2=\Delta\sin^2\theta$; it reduces to
Eqs.~\eqref{eq:Heta}--\eqref{eq:Htau-tk} on the equator. For $A=A(\eta)$ the
substitution~\eqref{eq:Ktransfer} is only the zeroth order: the transport equation
$\dd K/\dd\lambda=\{K,H\}+\partial_\eta K$ acquires an explicit source at order
$a^2\varepsilon$, $\varepsilon=A'/A$,
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\dd K}{\dd\eta}\bigg|_{\rm expl}=\varepsilon\,a^2\,S_1,\qquad
    S_1=2E_{\rm eff}^2\!\left[\cos^2\theta
        +\frac{M\cos2\theta\,p_\theta^2}{r\,DE_0^2}\right],
    \label{eq:S1}
\end{equation}
where $DE_0=r^2 w_{\rm eff}$. The counterterm $h$ solving $\{h,H_0\}=-S_1$
inherits the double pole at the \emph{freezing wall}
$r_w=2M/(1-E_{\rm eff}^2)$, so the dynamical quasi-constant is
\begin{equation}
    K^{\rm TK}(\eta)=K^{\rm Kerr}\!\big(E_{\rm eff}(\eta)\big)
        -a^2\!\int\!\varepsilon\,S_1\,\dd\eta,
    \label{eq:Kdyn}
\end{equation}
with closed toroidal averages
$\langle S_1\rangle=E_{\rm eff}^2(1-J^2/L^2)
+2ME_{\rm eff}^2|J|(L-|J|)/(r\,DE_0^2)$, $L^2=p_\theta^2+J^2/\sin^2\theta$. In
Kerr ($E>1$) the wall is absent; in Thakurta--Kerr with $E_{\rm eff}<1$ it enters
from the far field and advances inward as $A$ grows, becoming a \emph{global
attractor}: the bound $\tau$-libration ceases to exist, so the dynamical
quasi-constants live on scattering arcs ($E_{\rm eff}>1$) or on the $t$-branch.

\subsection{Semi-analytic first-order adiabatic orbit shape}
\label{sec:adiabatic}
The transport equation~\eqref{eq:Kdyn} controls the quasi-constant; the
\emph{orbit shape} $\varphi(r,A)$ itself admits a companion first-order adiabatic
form~\cite{BornFock1928,BenderOrszag1999} that is explicit up to a single higher
transcendental. Writing the frozen integrand as
$F(r;E_{\rm eff},J_{\rm eff})=\dd\varphi/\dd r$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-K} for the
$t$-branch, Eq.~\eqref{eq:tau-sextic} for $\tau$) and linearizing the conformal
weights of \emph{both} Kerr charges---$E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$ and $J_{\rm eff}=J/A$
carry weight $A^{-1}$ (for $u^{\rm TK}=u^{\rm Kerr}/A$,
$-g^{\rm TK}(u,\partial_\eta)=A\,E_{\rm eff}$ and
$g^{\rm TK}(u,\partial_\varphi)=A\,J_{\rm eff}$)---in
$A(\eta)=A_0(1+\varepsilon\eta+\dots)$, $\varepsilon=A'/A$, gives
\begin{equation}
    \varphi(r,A)=\underbrace{\varphi_0(r;E_{\rm eff},J_{\rm eff})}_{\text{frozen, Kleinian}}
    +\varepsilon\big[\mathcal{C}(r)+\psi(r)\big]+O(\varepsilon^2),
    \label{eq:phi-adiab}
\end{equation}
where the first-order coefficient is the elapsed-clock--weighted \emph{scaling}
derivative
$\delta\varphi=-\!\int\!(\Ehat\,\partial_E F+J\,\partial_J F)\,\eta\,\dd r
=-\!\int\!(E\partial_E+J\partial_J)F\,\eta\,\dd r$ (the $J\,\partial_J F$ term is
required by axisymmetric momentum conservation; Vaidya keeps only $\partial_m F$),
and $\eta(r)$
is the clock the orbit accumulates along the frozen slice: coordinate time
$\eta=t(r)$ on the $t$-branch, proper time $\eta=\tau(r)=\int\!L_\tau\,\dd r$ with
$L_\tau=\sqrt{Q/w}$, $Q=\Delta\varphi'^2-2a\beta\varphi'+r^2/(r^2+a^2)$, on the
$\tau$-branch (the extra $A^{-2}$ of the proper-time functional affects the
\emph{timing}, not the shape, so Eq.~\eqref{eq:phi-adiab} is common to both
branches).

The scaling derivative reduces to the same depth-one class as $\varphi_0$ itself, by
the classical reduction of abelian differentials to first/second
kind~\cite{BakerAbelian1897,GriffithsHarris1978}; $\partial_E F$ and $\partial_J F$
share the identical basis. Solving $(E\partial_E+J\partial_J)F=\dd_r[\mathcal{A}(r)/\mathcal{R}]
+\sum_{k=0}^{4}c_k\,r^k/\mathcal{R}$ for a degree-five numerator
$\mathcal{A}(r)=\sum_{i=0}^{5}a_ir^i$ and constants $c_k=\Ehat\,c_k^{E}+J\,c_k^{J}$
($\mathcal{R}$ the branch sextic radical, $\sqrt{R_6}$ or
$\sqrt{S}$) yields
\begin{equation}
    (E\partial_E+J\partial_J)\varphi_0=\frac{\mathcal{A}(r)}{\mathcal{R}}
    +\sum_{k=0}^{4}c_k\!\int\!\frac{r^k\,\dd r}{\mathcal{R}},
    \label{eq:dEphi0}
\end{equation}
with \emph{no} third-kind (horizon-pole) contribution: the second-kind reduction
closes with vanishing polar residues for both branches (verified to machine
precision), the $J\,\partial_J F$ term adding only its coefficients $c_k^{J}$
(\texttt{ThakurtaMetric/conformal\_source\_Jterm.py}). Hence
$\mathcal{C}(r)=-\tfrac12(E\partial_E+J\partial_J)\varphi_0\,\eta(r)$ is
a product of depth-one objects, closed in the Kleinian/abelian class.

\emph{Clock differentials.} The clock $\eta$ enters $\mathcal{C}$ through its
differential, closed on the branch curve. On the $\tau$-branch the proper-time
density reduces to a rational second-kind differential,
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\dd\tau}{\dd r}=\frac{r^2(r-2M)}{\sqrt{S}},
    \label{eq:clock-tau}
\end{equation}
the frame-dragging term cancelling identically ($10^{-16}$). The $t$-branch
coordinate-time density is \emph{also entirely on-curve}: the identity
$(r-2M)Q_2+DE(J(r-2M)+2Ma)^2=E^2r^3\Delta$ (with $E^2-f=DE/r$) collapses the Randers
wind, $n_t\alpha=E^2r^3/(f\sqrt{R_6})$, and
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\dd t}{\dd r}=\frac{\rho_t(r)}{\sqrt{R_6}},\quad
    \rho_t=\frac{E^2r^3-(2Ma/r)\mathcal K}{f}=P_3(r)+\frac{R_\Delta(r)}{\Delta},
    \label{eq:clock-t}
\end{equation}
the ergosphere pole $r=2M$ removable, third-kind poles only at the horizons. The
second-kind part $P_3=E^2r^3+2E^2Mr^2+4E^2M^2r+8E^2M^3-2(E^2-1)JMa$ gives the clock
vector $b=(8E^2M^3-2(E^2-1)JMa,4E^2M^2,2E^2M,E^2,0)$ ($t$-analogue of the $\tau$-branch
$b=(0,0,-2M,1,0)$), and $R_\Delta$ is the degree-one horizon numerator (explicit in
\texttt{reproduce\_reductions.py}). \emph{Neither} branch leaves the curve (verified
against the Hamiltonian flow to $10^{-10}$); the $t$-branch merely adds a third-kind
horizon letter---the apparent Doran double cover of a first analysis was spurious.

structure~\eqref{eq:phi-adiab} and differ only in the branch curve and the clock.

\emph{The correction $\psi$.} By parts and the shuffle identity~\cite{Chen1977}
$\rho+\tilde\rho=\partial_E\varphi_0\,\eta$
($\rho=\int\partial_E\varphi_0\,\dd\eta$,
$\tilde\rho=\int\eta\,\partial_E F\,\dd r$) is closed, and the antisymmetric
remainder
\begin{equation}
    \psi(r)=\tfrac12\Ehat\,(\rho-\tilde\rho)
    \label{eq:psi-irr}
\end{equation}
is a depth-two iterated integral. Expand $\partial_E\varphi_0$ and $\eta$ in the
cohomology basis $U_k=\int r^k/\mathcal{R}\,\dd r$: the reduction
$\partial_E F=N/\mathcal{R}^3$, $N=EJ\,r^4(r-2M)^2 DE$ (the horizon factor $\Delta$
cancels) gives $\partial_E\varphi_0=\mathcal{A}/\mathcal{R}+\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k U_k$,
and on $\tau$ $\eta=U_3-2M\,U_2$, i.e.\ $b=(0,0,-2M,1,0)$. The angular piece
$\partial_J F=N_J/\mathcal{R}^3$ reduces in the same basis and threads through the
identical shuffle, adding $J\,\partial_J\varphi_0$ to the energy term. The complete
first-order coefficient
$\delta\varphi=\mathcal{C}+\psi=-\int(\Ehat\,\partial_E F+J\,\partial_J F)\,\eta\,\dd r$
reorganizes, with no term discarded, into ($C_k\equiv\Ehat c_k^{E}+J c_k^{J}$)
\begin{equation}
    \delta\varphi=
    \underbrace{\tfrac12\!\sum_{k<j}Q_{kj}\,W_{kj}}_{\text{weight two}}
    -\underbrace{\eta\big(\tfrac{\mathcal{A}}{\mathcal{R}}
    +\tfrac12\!\sum_k C_k U_k\big)}_{\text{clock}\times\text{abelian}}
    +\underbrace{\sum_j b_j\,\mathcal{I}_j}_{\text{elementary}},
    \label{eq:psi-split}
\end{equation}
$Q_{kj}=C_k b_j-C_j b_k$, $W_{kj}=\int(U_k\dd U_j-U_j\dd U_k)$ the genus-two
polylogarithms, $\mathcal{I}_j=\int\mathcal{A}\,r^j/\mathcal{R}^2\,\dd r$ elementary
(rational, evaluating to logarithms). Every piece is explicit---the second group a
product of the clock with the reduced abelian integrals, the third an elementary
logarithmic remainder; none is spurious. The $Q_{kj}$ are \emph{algebraic}
(rational in $M,a,E,J$ through the $c_k$); we verified~\eqref{eq:psi-split} to
$4\times10^{-14}$ with all coefficients given, not fitted, and $Q_{01}=0$ (the clock
has no first-kind part). On the $t$-branch the same holds with
$\mathcal{R}^2=R_6$: the clock is likewise on-curve, its second-kind vector $b$
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:clock-t}] feeding the $W_{kj}$ of $R_6$ and its horizon part
$R_\Delta/\Delta$ the third-kind $\ln\sigma$-quotients at $r_\pm$---no double cover.
The full $t$-branch assembly reproduces the direct
$-\Ehat\!\int\!\partial_E F\,t\,\dd r$ to $9\times10^{-13}$.
The $W_{kj}$ are the genus-two hyperelliptic
polylogarithms of $y^2=\mathcal{R}^2$, weight-two iterated
integrals~\cite{Chen1977} in the (elliptic/higher-genus) polylogarithm class of
Beilinson--Levin and Brown--Levin~\cite{BeilinsonLevin1994,BrownLevin2011}: they do
not reduce to a weight-one Kleinian $\zeta,\sigma$
form~\cite{BakerAbelian1897,BuchstaberEnolskii1997}, since for genus two the Abel
image of single points lies on the theta divisor $\Theta=W_{g-1}$~\cite{MumfordTata}
(where $\zeta,\wp$ are singular). The single third-kind piece---the clock's dipole at
infinity, residue $\rho_0=M/(E^2-1)^{3/2}$---integrates to
\begin{equation}
    L(u)=\log\frac{\theta[\delta](u-e_+)}{\theta[\delta](u-e_-)}
    \label{eq:thirdkind-theta}
\end{equation}
(Fay prime form~\cite{Fay1973}, odd $\delta$, $e_\pm$ the Abel images of infinity;
$L(u(r))$ differentiated back reproduces the third-kind differential to
$5\times10^{-8}$). Like $\mathrm{Li}_2$, the $W_{kj}$ are their own closed form
(Kronecker--Eisenstein series~\cite{BeilinsonLevin1994}); the weight-two content is genuine and (conjecturally) irreducible, the coefficients
algebraic. Explicitly the pole coefficients are $g_0=1/\sqrt{E^2-1}$,
$g_1=(2E^2-3)/(E^2-1)^{3/2}$ ($E$-only), with $g_2$ and the $c_k$ rational in
$(a,J)$ too, and $T_{\rm alg}=P(r)+\sum_i\mathrm{res}_i\ln(r-r_i)$ over the roots of
$S$; the genus-two dilogarithm~\eqref{eq:thirdkind-theta} is evaluated as a
Kronecker--Eisenstein nome ($q$-)series that, after Siegel reduction $\tau'=-\tau^{-1}$
(all nomes ${<}1$), converges geometrically ($N{=}4$ matches to $10^{-13}$). All symbolic coefficients,
the separatrix residues, and the $\Jc$-tracking theorem (below) are independently
reproduced in Mathematica (native $\wp$, Gröbner reduction, Laurent extraction) to
$10^{-16}$--$10^{-38}$.

Table~\ref{tab:adiab-valid} compares the hybrid
form~\eqref{eq:phi-adiab}--\eqref{eq:psi-irr} (closed $\mathcal{C}$ plus numerical
$\psi$) against the directly integrated non-autonomous coefficient
$-\Ehat\!\int\partial_E F\,\eta\,\dd r$, for both branches ($M=1$, $a=0.9$,
$\Ehat=1.4$; $t$-branch $J=6$, $\tau$-branch scattering $J=2.5$). The agreement is
at the discretization floor and scales linearly with $\varepsilon$, confirming
that the split is an exact reorganization (integration by parts), not an
approximation. Figure~\ref{fig:phi-closed} shows the same comparison as orbit

Table~\ref{tab:adiab-valid} certifies only the \emph{algebraic} identity; it does not
test the adiabatic form against the true dynamics. Integrating the actual
non-autonomous optical-metric geodesic (the branch-Hamiltonian flow with
$E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$, $J_{\rm eff}=J/A$) versus $\varphi_0+\varepsilon\,\delta\varphi$
gives a \emph{physical} accuracy $\sim2\%$, linear in $\varepsilon$, far above the
algebraic floor (Fig.~\ref{fig:phi-true-dynamic}). Linearizing the extended
Hamiltonian $\mathcal H=p_s+H=0$ about the frozen extremal---with $\lambda$ the
elapsed clock measured \emph{from the free-arrival endpoint} (where $H=0$ by
transversality), oriented inward---gives $\dd p_\eta/\dd\lambda=\Theta H$ with
$p_\eta=0$ there, hence $\delta p_r=(\lambda\Theta H-S)/H_{p_r}$ and
\begin{equation}
    \begin{aligned}
    &\frac{\delta\varphi}{\varepsilon}=\int\!\Big[\frac{\partial_{p_r}G}{H_{p_r}}
    \big(\lambda\,\Theta H-S\big)-\lambda\,\Theta G\Big]\dd r,\\
    &G=\frac{H_J}{H_{p_r}}=\varphi',\quad
    S(\lambda)=\!\int_0^{\lambda}\!\Theta H\,\dd\lambda',
    \end{aligned}
    \label{eq:adiab-exact}
\end{equation}
with $\Theta\equiv E_{\rm eff}\partial_{E_{\rm eff}}+J_{\rm eff}\partial_{J_{\rm eff}}$
(it differentiates only the dynamical charges $E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$, $J_{\rm eff}=J/A$;
the rail invariants $\Ehat,J_{\rm TK}$ are conserved and inert under $\Theta$; for
Vaidya $\Theta=m\partial_m$),
closing the correction to $O(\varepsilon^2)$; $S$ is \emph{terminally anchored}
($S=0$ at the free-arrival endpoint, so $\delta p_r,\delta J\to0$ there) and the
reduction holds on compact sub-arcs bounded away from the turning point ($H_{p_r}\to0$).
Verified numerically (slope~$\approx2$). This splits into an on-shell part and an
off-shell part driven by the slow-time costate ($H_{\rm ext}=p_\eta+H=0$,
$H=-p_\eta\sim O(\varepsilon)$): the latter, $-\!\int(\partial_{p_r}G/H_{p_r})S\,\dd r$,
is a depth-two iterated integral (a length-two iterated Abelian integral of the same
class). The
$\tfrac12(E_{\rm eff}\partial_{E_{\rm eff}}+J_{\rm eff}\partial_{J_{\rm eff}})\varphi_0\,\eta$
term is the leading approximation to
Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}, accurate to $\sim2\%$; its exact off-shell closed form lives
in the same genus-two class as $\mathcal{C}+\psi$.

\section{Equatorial closed forms and the ergosphere}
\label{sec:closed}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------

All closed forms in this section and in App.~\ref{app:explicit} are obtained at
\emph{frozen conformal factor}: constant $A$, equivalently stationary Kerr with
the substitution $E\to E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$ (and $J\to J/A$, spin $a$ retained) in every
parameter (Sec.~\ref{sec:thakurta}). They are therefore the exact
\emph{instantaneous} orbit shapes on a Thakurta--Kerr slice of given $A$; the
running $A(\eta)$ makes the full problem non-autonomous---the quasi-constant
drift of Sec.~\ref{sec:quasiconst}, $K^{\rm TK}(\eta)=K^{\rm Kerr}(E_{\rm
eff}(\eta))+O(A'/A)$---so a genuinely time-dependent trajectory is recovered by
letting $E_{\rm eff}=E_{\rm eff}(\eta)$ run through this family, not by a single
autonomous curve.

\subsection{Separatrix in Weierstrass functions}
\label{sec:separatrix}
From the doranTau factorization $\Delta-\Jc^2 w=f(r^2+c^2)$, $c=a/E$
\cite{RosignoliKerr}, the
equatorial $\tau$-separatrix $\varphi(r)$ is genus~1, closed in the
Weierstrass functions $\wp,\sigma,\zeta$
\cite{WhittakerWatson,DLMF,Hackmann2008,Hackmann2010}: the angle is linear in the
uniformizing variable $z(r)=\wp^{-1}(A/r+B)$ plus two $\sigma$-ratios
[App.~\ref{app:explicit}, Eq.~\eqref{eq:sep-phi}], regular \emph{through} the
ergosphere because the factor $r^2+c^2$ divides the turning quartic and removes
$\re$ from the radicand. The Boyer--Lindquist form winds logarithmically at the
horizon; the Doran shift---a second, quasi-lemniscatic elliptic
curve---cancels it, so the ergosphere-penetrating separatrix
$\varphi_D=\varphi_{\rm BL}-\varphi_{\rm shift}$ is closed on two elliptic curves.

What controls the reduction is the \emph{genus} of the turning radical, not the
rationality of $\mathcal K$. Being Riemannian-pure
(Sec.~\ref{sec:thakurta}, the gravitomagnetic wind cancels), the $\tau$-branch
factorizes as doranTau---the factor $r^2+c^2$ dividing the quartic---dropping a
generically genus-2 reduction to genus~1. Generic $t$-branch orbits carry the
wind and have the sextic radical
$\mathcal R(r)=r\,Q_2(r)\,[(E^2-1)r+2M]$ (genus~2, hyperelliptic), with no such
cancellation. The $t$-brachistochrone nonetheless has its own genus-1 closed-form
separatrix, by a \emph{different} mechanism: at the marginal-capture (saddle-node)
momentum $J_c^-$ [Eq.~\eqref{eq:Jc-minus}] the sextic develops a \emph{double
root}, $\mathcal R=(r-r_*)^2 Q_4$ with $Q_4$ quartic (whereas the prograde
ergosphere crossing $J_+^t$ has only a simple root and stays genus~2). The double
root drops the radical to $Q_4$, and $\varphi(r)$ closes in the same
Weierstrass $\wp,\sigma,\zeta$ form as the $\tau$-separatrix
(App.~\ref{app:explicit}). Both separatrices are thus genus~1---the $\tau$ one
via the doranTau factorization, the $t$ one via a double root---while generic
(non-separatrix) $t$-orbits are genuinely genus~2, closed only in genus-2
Kleinian functions.
The Kerr geodesic structure and its constants are classical
\cite{Carter1968,Chandrasekhar}. Validated end-to-end to
$2.8\times10^{-10}$ through the ergosphere, and general across parameters.

\subsection{Trichotomy and the ergosphere cusp}
\label{sec:trichotomy}
For the equatorial $\tau$-branch, $\dd\varphi/\dd r\sim K\sqrt{r-\re}$ as
$r\to\re$ (because $\sqrt f\to0$ while the turning radicand stays finite),
giving
\begin{equation}
    \varphi-\varphi_e \sim \tfrac{2}{3}K\,(r-\re)^{3/2},
    \label{eq:cusp}
\end{equation}
a cusp with radial tangent. The trichotomy in $|J|$ (sign of $a^2-J^2E^2$):
smooth periapsis above $\re$ ($|J|>\Jc$); marginal contact with $\re$
($|J|=\Jc=a/E$, separatrix); cusp reflection at $\re$ ($|J|<\Jc$). Only the
\emph{prograde} marginal $J=+\Jc$ actually crosses: inside $\re$ frame dragging
forces co-rotation ($\dd\varphi/\dd\lambda>0$), so the retrograde marginal
$J=-\Jc$, which would counter-rotate, reflects with a cusp instead. The crossing
(prograde) branch is continued through $\re$ to the horizon in Doran
(horizon-penetrating) coordinates
\cite{Doran2000,Natario2009,MartelPoisson2001},
$\varphi_D=\varphi_{\rm BL}-\varphi_{\rm shift}$.

\paragraph{Retrograde branch and the $\tau/t$ asymmetry.} The trichotomy above is
a $\tau$-branch statement. The set that \emph{reaches} $\re$ is symmetric in the
sign of $J$---the doranTau factorization depends on $J$ only through $J^2$, so the
ingoing orbit reaches $\re$ for $|J|\le\Jc=a/E$, prograde and retrograde sharing
the same threshold despite frame dragging---but the set that \emph{penetrates} is
not: only the single prograde $+\Jc$ crosses, the co-rotation constraint breaking
the $\pm J$ symmetry at the wall. The $t$-branch is not protected. Its turning function (from
$H_\eta=0$ at $p_r=0$, with $b=2Ma/r$) is
\begin{equation}
    N(r)=\big(\bar P-Jb\bar v^2\big)^2-J^2\Delta\bar v^2,
    \label{eq:tbranch-turn}
\end{equation}
which is only linear-plus-quadratic in $J$. At the ergosphere
$N(\re)=\bar P_e\,(\bar P_e-2aJ)$ with
$\bar P_e=\bar P(\re)=4M^2+2a^2+a^2/E^2$, so the $t$-orbit crosses $\re$ at the
single \emph{prograde} value
\begin{equation}
    J_+^{t}=\frac{\bar P_e}{2a}=\frac{4M^2+2a^2+a^2/E^2}{2a};
    \label{eq:Jplus-t}
\end{equation}
there is no retrograde $r_e$-crossing. Instead the retrograde $t$-capture is
bounded by the \emph{marginal} (double-root) condition $N=N'=0$ at a radius
$r_*>\re$---the massive analogue of the retrograde photon sphere---which closes
to
\begin{equation}
    J_c^{-}=-\,\frac{\bar P}{\bar v\,(\sqrt\Delta-b\bar v)}\bigg|_{r_*}<0 .
    \label{eq:Jc-minus}
\end{equation}
For $a=0.9$, $E=1.2$ (and $A=1$) these give $J_+^{t}=3.43$ and $J_c^{-}=-8.05$
($r_*=3.51$): the $t$-branch captures the wide, asymmetric band
$J\in[J_c^{-},J_+^{t}]$, far more capture-prone on the retrograde side. A
moderately retrograde orbit ($J=-5$, say) is therefore captured on the
$t$-branch but scattered on the $\tau$-branch (Fig.~\ref{fig:master-pen}). For
constant $A$ the results carry
over by $E\to\Ehat/A$, $J\to J/A$ (spin $a$ retained); in particular
$\Jc^{\tau}=sA^2/\Ehat$.

\subsection{Plunge inversion: rotational and conformal}
\label{sec:inversion}
Two mechanisms invert the $t/\tau$ ordering. Rotationally, in the $(a,J)$ plane,
$\rho(r_*)^2=H_{t0}/H_{\tau0}$, extending the stationary Kerr analysis
\cite{RosignoliKerr}. Conformally, the inversion locus is
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{cond}(r,A)=A^2 b\,Q-\bar P(\Ehat-A^2 f)=0,\quad
    Q=b\bar v^2+\sqrt{\bar v^2\Delta},
    \label{eq:condinv}
\end{equation}
with $\mathrm{cond}(1)<0$ and $\mathrm{cond}(\Af)=\Ehat r\Delta(\Ehat-1)/(r-2M)$.
Under $\Ehat>1$, $r>2M$, $\Delta>0$ (exterior scattering) the latter is $>0$, so by
the intermediate value theorem a root $A_{\rm inv}\in(1,\Af)$ exists, reachable when
$A_{\rm inv}<\Af$ (far field $A_{\rm inv}\to\sqrt{\Ehat}$). This is a
\emph{conditional} existence: for $\Ehat\le1$ no such root is guaranteed.

\subsection{Fixed-endpoint comparison and protocol dependence}
\label{sec:bvp}
The statement ``branch $X$ plunges deeper'' is protocol dependent. With
\emph{fixed initial data} (same $r_0,J,p_r$) the $t$-branch is deeper
($r_{\min}^t<r_{\min}^\tau$). With \emph{fixed endpoints} (the genuine
two-point brachistochrone), the two branches join the same $A,B$ but are
distinct paths, and the ordering reverses: $r_{\min}^\tau<r_{\min}^t$. The
optical-index ratio $n_t/n_\tau=E/f>1$ penalizes deep radii more heavily for
the $t$-branch, so the $t$-brachistochrone bows outward while the $\tau$-branch
dips. This must be specified when comparing depths.

    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{Immagini/fig_bvp_estremi_fissi.png}
    \caption{Fixed-endpoint $t$ and $\tau$ brachistochrones (Schwarzschild,
    $E=1.2$): symmetric (top) and asymmetric (bottom) endpoints. Both branches
    join the same $A,B$; the $\tau$-branch dips deeper.}
    \label{fig:bvp}
\end{figure}

Crucially, neither driver of same-launch inversion survives the
fixed-endpoint constraint. Scanning the spin $a$ over the entire
Kerr flow with fixed symmetric endpoints $(r_0,\pm\Phi)$ leaves
$\Delta r=r_{\min}^t-r_{\min}^\tau$ strictly positive
($\Delta r\in[+0.45,+2.06]$ on $a\in[0.05,0.95]$); the map is nearly flat
in $a$, its gradient set almost entirely by the endpoint angle
$\Phi$ (encoded by $J$). The conformal scan (via the transfer
$E_{\rm eff}=\hat E/A$) is likewise everywhere positive
($\Delta r\in[+0.002,+3.711]$). The pointwise bound $n_t/n_\tau=E/f>1$ does not by
itself order the minima of two distinct functionals (at \emph{fixed} $J$ the order is
opposite; the fixed-endpoint result is a reversal via the angle map $J\mapsto\Phi$),
but it reduces cleanly: parametrizing each branch by its turning radius $r_{\min}$ and
setting $\Phi_{\rm br}(r_{\min})=\int_{r_{\min}}^{r_0}F_{\rm br}(r;J_{\rm
br}(r_{\min}))\dd r$, two lemmas (verified over $a\in[0.3,0.9]$, $E\in[1.05,1.5]$)
close it: (A) at matched $r_{\min}$ the $t$-integrand dominates pointwise,
$F_t>F_\tau$---an \emph{algebraic} inequality since $J_{t,\tau}(r_{\min})$ are
algebraic---so $\Phi_t>\Phi_\tau$; (B) $\Phi_{\rm br}$ is strictly decreasing. Then
$\Phi_\tau(r_{\min}^\tau)=\Phi_t(r_{\min}^t)>\Phi_\tau(r_{\min}^t)$ gives
$r_{\min}^\tau<r_{\min}^t$. Lemma~(A) is proven in full: reducing
$F_t^2-F_\tau^2=(r\,DE/\Delta^2)N_G/(Q_2P_\tau)$ modulo the turning conditions gives
$N_G=(r-r_{\min})[4r\Delta/(r_{\min}-2M)]\,W(r)$ with $W$ \emph{linear} in $r$, so
(A) $\Leftrightarrow W>0$; a linear $W$ with $W(r_{\min})=(r_{\min}-2M)aDE_{\min}(J^\ast-J_t)>0$
and slope $w_1=aDE_{\min}(J^{\ast\ast}-J_t)>0$ is positive for $r>r_{\min}$. Both
follow because the prograde root $J_t$ of $Q_2(r_{\min},\cdot)$ (which opens downward,
$A=-(r_{\min}-2M)DE_{\min}<0$, negative vertex) lies below $J^\ast,J^{\ast\ast}>0$,
since $Q_2(r_{\min},J^\ast)$ and $Q_2(r_{\min},J^{\ast\ast})$ are manifestly negative
(\texttt{no\_inversion\_reduction.py}). Thus (A) is a theorem. Lemma~(B),
$\Phi_\tau'(r_{\min})<0$, is \emph{conditional}: $\Phi_\tau$ peaks at
$r_{\rm pk}\approx3M$ (strong-deflection threshold) and decreases for
$r_{\min}>r_{\rm pk}$. In the effective-potential form
$\Phi_\tau=\sqrt{V_{\min}}\int K/\sqrt{V-V_{\min}}\,\dd r$ ($V=r\Delta/DE$), the
substitution $V=V_{\min}+\sigma^2$ and one IBP give
$\Phi_\tau'\propto(V_0-2V_{\min})W(V_{\min})/\Sigma+2\int_0^\Sigma W'(V_{\min}+\sigma^2)g\,\dd\sigma$
with $W=K/V'$ ($W'<0$) and $g=(V_0-2V_{\min})\sigma/\Sigma+V_{\min}-\sigma^2>0$ on
$[0,\Sigma)$, so the integral is manifestly negative; for $V(r_0)\le2V(r_{\min})$
(grazing) the boundary is $\le0$ too and (B) is \emph{proven in closed form}. The same
closed form (an IBP identity $\sqrt{V_{\min}}\Phi_\tau'=W(V_0)(V_0-2V_{\min})/\Sigma+
\int_{V_{\min}}^{V_0}W'(t)(2V_{\min}-t)/\sqrt{t-V_{\min}}\,\dd t$) plus convexity of $W$
extends the proof to $V(r_0)\le4V(r_{\min})$, hence to all $r_0\le R_\ast(E)$. In the
static limit $W(V)=\tfrac1{2V}-\tfrac1{2b^{3/2}V^{3/2}}+O(V^{-2})$ ($b=E^2-1$), so
$\Phi_\tau=\arctan\!\sqrt{V_0/V_{\min}-1}-b^{-3/2}\sqrt{1/V_{\min}-1/V_0}+\cdots$, a
single non-degenerate maximum ($\Phi_\tau''(V_{\rm pk})=-\tfrac12 b^{9/4}V_0^{-5/4}<0$),
so (B) holds for all large $r_0$. For intermediate $r_0$ the single-crossing property is
established by a validated interval-arithmetic certificate, completed for $r_0=10M$ over
the whole continuum and on a grid $E\in\{1.2,1.6,2.5\}\times r_0\in\{8,12\}M$
(\texttt{no\_inversion\_schwarzschild\_CAP\_grid.py}). A uniform symbolic proof for all
$(a,E,r_0)$ remains open (the deciding inequality is tight at the transcendental
$r_{\rm pk}$); we thus state the fixed-endpoint no-inversion as a theorem for
$r_0\le R_\ast(E)$ and, in the static case, as $r_0\to\infty$, machine-certified at
representative $r_0$, and a numerically supported conjecture in full generality. The
fixed-endpoint turning points ($r_{\min}^{\tau,t}\gtrsim4.7M$) lie in the certified
scattering regime.

\subsection{The breathing indicatrix: a unified adiabatic theorem}
\label{sec:unified}
The Thakurta--Kerr and Vaidya corrections [Eqs.~\eqref{eq:psi-split},
\eqref{eq:vaidya-deltaphi}] are one statement, and the indicatrix ellipse of
Sec.~\ref{sec:indicatrix} is why. For a one-parameter family of rail indicatrices
with frozen integrand $F(r;\lambda)$ on the genus-two curve $y^2=S(r;\lambda)$
($\lambda=$ scale $a$, mass $m$, or conformal $A$), a slow drift
$\lambda=\lambda(\text{clock})$ gives
\begin{equation}
    \varphi=\varphi_0(r;\lambda)+\dot\lambda\!\int\!\partial_\lambda F\,
    (\text{clock})\,\dd r
    =\varphi_0+\dot\lambda\Big[\sum_{k<j} q_{kj}W_{kj}+(\text{elem.})\Big],
    \label{eq:unified}
\end{equation}
with $q_{kj}$ algebraic (via $\partial_\lambda F=N_\lambda/S^{3/2}$) and
$W_{kj}=\int(U_j\dd U_k-U_k\dd U_j)$ length-two iterated Abelian integrals of the frozen
curve. Equation~\eqref{eq:unified} is the \emph{on-shell} component; the complete
first-order term is Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}, whose off-shell (costate) piece is of
the same class. Within the on-shell component the transcendence structure is universal. The geometries are distinguished \emph{not}
by which datum varies---in each, \emph{both} centre and semi-axes breathe,
$\partial_\lambda F=\partial_c F(\partial_\lambda c)+\partial_R F(\partial_\lambda
R)$ with both terms nonzero (Vaidya: $\partial_m c_r=-2/r$,
$\partial_m R_r=E[r(2m+(E^2-1)r)]^{-1/2}$, etc.; Thakurta--Kerr via
$\partial_A E_{\rm eff}=-\Ehat/A^2$)---but by the wind \emph{orientation} (Vaidya's
radial centre-wind $c_r=f-\Ehat^2$ vs.\ Thakurta--Kerr's azimuthal frame-drag) and,
decisively for the letter, the \emph{clock} singularity: $v$ diverges at the horizon
$r=2m$ (Vaidya $\dd\log$ \emph{there}); $t,\tau$ grow at large $r$ (Thakurta--Kerr
third-kind dipole \emph{at infinity}). FLRW is the degenerate circle ($c=0$,
isotropic $R$): branches coincide, polylogs collapse to genus zero. The thread ``circle $\to$
radial-wind ellipse $\to$ angular-wind ellipse'' is the ladder of weight-two letters
the breathing switches on, on one hyperelliptic backbone.

\paragraph{Domain of validity.} The statement is a \emph{first-order} adiabatic
theorem resting on two \emph{exact} lemmas---the algebraic reduction
$\partial_\lambda F=N_\lambda/S^{3/2}$ with the unique rational $c_k$ from the
polynomial identity~\eqref{eq:reduction-identity} (nonsingular when $S$ has distinct
roots, i.e.\ off the separatrix), and the by-parts assembly~\eqref{eq:psi-split} via
the shuffle relation---so the closed form is rigorous, not fitted, where those hold.
Two honest caveats: it is $O(\dot\lambda)$ (the next order needs a further reduction);
and it \emph{fails at the turning points} $S(r)=0$, where
$\partial_\lambda F\sim N_\lambda/S^{3/2}$ diverges and $\dot\lambda\,\delta\varphi$ is
not uniformly valid---the closed forms hold on the smooth arcs and are joined across
turning points by the standard WKB connection formulae. On the separatrix the genus
drops and the reduction degenerates to the elliptic (Bloch--Wigner) case, a controlled
limit. Within these usual first-order WKB bounds this closed form is the exact
\emph{on-shell component} of the first-order response; the complete first-order term is
Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}, adding the off-shell (costate $p_\eta$) displacement of the
momentum shell (a $\sim2\%$ correction, Fig.~\ref{fig:phi-true-dynamic}).

\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:concl}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
The non-stationary brachistochrone is a well-posed controlled-rail problem: the
invariant $-u\cdot W=\Ehat$ is legitimate via Pontryagin's principle, and the
selector $W$ is fixed canonically by the Killing--CKV--Kodama hierarchy, with
the Kodama energy conserved along the rail even when free fall does not conserve
it. FLRW is the degenerate base---freezing without branch splitting---while the
mass breaks the $t/\tau$ symmetry through its radial gravitational gradient (the
spin instead driving the rotational plunge inversion). On
the equatorial sector we obtained closed-form separatrices in Weierstrass
functions, continued through the ergosphere in Doran coordinates, and an analytic
cusp theorem for the $|J|<\Jc$ reflection. The plunge inversion resolves into a
three-way picture: rotation and the conformal factor invert the $t/\tau$
ordering under a same-launch comparison, while in the ingoing-Vaidya model the
evaporative sign change is absent. The fixed-endpoint no-inversion is a \emph{theorem}
for $r_0\le R_\ast(E)$ and, in the static case, as $r_0\to\infty$; it is
machine-certified at representative $r_0$ and a numerically supported \emph{conjecture}
in general. Slow drift of the metric yields first-order corrections whose
\emph{on-shell} component is a closed form in length-two iterated Abelian integrals on
the frozen spectral curve---unified across the three geometries, the weight-two letter
migrating from a third-kind dipole at infinity (Thakurta--Kerr) to a $\dd\log$ at the
horizon (Vaidya), collapsing on the separatrix to an elliptic (Bloch--Wigner)
dilogarithm; the complete first-order term additionally carries an off-shell (costate)
piece of the same class, derived from the extended Hamiltonian and verified against the
true non-autonomous flow. Outlook: the
exact Sultana--Dyer source
\cite{SultanaDyer2005}, the McVittie/Schwarzschild--de Sitter limits
\cite{McVittie1933,Kottler1918,Kaloper2010} with their photon surfaces
\cite{ClaudelViracVirbhadra2001}, escape maps, and freezing-surface dynamics.

\subsection{Explicit closed forms}
\label{app:explicit}

All expressions below are at \emph{constant (frozen) conformal factor} $A$:
stationary Kerr with $E\to E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$, $J\to J/A$ (spin $a$ retained). They give
the instantaneous orbit shape on a Thakurta--Kerr slice; the time dependence
enters only through $E_{\rm eff}(\eta)$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:quasiconst}).

\paragraph{Equatorial $\tau$-branch separatrix in Weierstrass functions.} On the
$\tau$-separatrix $J=\Jc$ the doranTau factorization $\Delta-\Jc^2 w=f(r^2+c^2)$,
$c=a/E$, makes the
turning quartic $Q_4=r\big((E^2-1)r+2M\big)(r^2+c^2)$, and the factor $r^2+c^2$
divides $Q_4$ so that only the horizon poles survive. Writing
\begin{gather}
    A=\frac{Ma^2}{2E^2},\qquad B=\frac{a^2(E^2-1)}{12E^2},\notag\\
    g_2=\frac{a^4(E^2-1)^2}{12E^4}-\frac{a^2M^2}{E^2},\notag\\
    g_3=\frac{a^4\big[36E^2M^2(1-E^2)-a^2(E^2-1)^3\big]}{216E^6},
    \label{eq:wp-invariants}
\end{gather}
the uniformizing variable is $z(r)=\wp^{-1}\!\big(A/r+B;g_2,g_3\big)
=\int_0^r\dd r'/\sqrt{Q_4}$, the Abel map of the turning quartic $Q_4$: it is the
elliptic argument in which the radial motion linearizes, so that $r$ is recovered
by $A/r+B=\wp(z)$ and the physical range $r\in[r_{\min},\infty)$ maps to a
segment of $z$. With $r_\pm=M\pm\sqrt{M^2-a^2}$ the horizons,
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \alpha_\pm&=\frac{r_\pm\big((E^2-1)r_\pm+2M\big)}{r_\pm-r_\mp},\\
    \lambda_\pm&=\frac{\alpha_\pm}{\sqrt{Q_4(r_\pm)}},\qquad v_\pm=z(r_\pm),
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
$\wp(v_\pm)=A/r_\pm+B$, and
$\Lambda_0=(E^2-1)-\alpha_+/r_+-\alpha_-/r_-+2\lambda_+\zeta(v_+)+2\lambda_-\zeta(v_-)$,
the angle is linear in the uniformizing variable $z\equiv z(r)$ plus two
Weierstrass-$\sigma$ ratios,
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
    \varphi(r)=\frac{a}{E}\Big[&\Lambda_0\,z(r)
      +\lambda_+\ln\frac{\sigma(z-v_+)}{\sigma(z+v_+)}\\
      &+\lambda_-\ln\frac{\sigma(z-v_-)}{\sigma(z+v_-)}\Big]+\text{const}.
\end{split}
    \label{eq:sep-phi}
\end{equation}
Because $Q_4(2M)=4M^2(4E^2M^2+a^2)>0$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:sep-phi} is regular
\emph{through} the ergosphere $\re=2M$; the only singularities are the
third-kind logarithms at the horizons. The conformal case is
$E\to E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$ in every parameter.

\paragraph{Doran continuation through the horizon.} The Boyer--Lindquist form
$\varphi(r)$ winds logarithmically at $\rp$ (the standard BL artifact); adding the
Doran shift $\dd\varphi_{\rm shift}=2Mar\,\dd r/(\Delta\sqrt{C_3})$ with the cubic
$C_3=2Mr(r^2+a^2)$ regularizes it. This is a \emph{second} elliptic curve, in
clean Weierstrass form $r(z)=(2/M)\wp(z;g_2,g_3)$ with $g_2=-M^2a^2$, $g_3=0$
(quasi-lemniscatic), giving
$\varphi_{\rm shift}=\tfrac{a}{r_+-r_-}\big(B_+-B_-\big)$,
$B_\pm=2\zeta(v_\pm)z+\ln[\sigma(z-v_\pm)/\sigma(z+v_\pm)]$. With $\dd\varphi_{\rm
shift}$ as above (positive residue at $\rp$), the log residues of $\varphi_{\rm BL}$
and $\varphi_{\rm shift}$ are \emph{equal} ($1.0324=1.0324$ at $a=0.9,E=1.2,J=3$),
so they cancel under \emph{subtraction}: the single consistent sign is
$\varphi_D=\varphi_{\rm BL}-\varphi_{\rm shift}$, used throughout, closed on two
elliptic curves.

\paragraph{General $\tau$-branch $\varphi(r)$: genus-2 Kleinian form.} Off the
separatrix, for \emph{arbitrary} $J$, the $\tau$ integrand
$\dd\varphi/\dd r=\mathcal K_\tau(r)/\sqrt{\mathcal S(r)}$ is genus~2, with the
sextic
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal S(r)=r(r-2M)\big[(E^2-1)r+2M\big]
      \big[r\Delta-J^2\big((E^2-1)r+2M\big)\big]
    \label{eq:tau-sextic}
\end{equation}
(six generically simple branch points, hyperelliptic) and the rational numerator
$\mathcal K_\tau=Jr(r-2M)\big[(E^2-1)r+2M\big]/\Delta$. Partial fractions split it
into a first-kind (holomorphic) part and third-kind poles at the horizons
$r_\pm=M\pm\sqrt{M^2-a^2}$,
\begin{equation}
    \varphi(r)=c_1\!\int\!\frac{r\,\dd r}{\sqrt{\mathcal S}}
      +c_0\!\int\!\frac{\dd r}{\sqrt{\mathcal S}}
      +\sum_{\pm}\alpha_\pm\!\int\!\frac{\dd r}{(r-r_\pm)\sqrt{\mathcal S}},
    \label{eq:tau-genus2}
\end{equation}
with closed-form coefficients (in $M,a,E,J$)
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    &c_1=J(E^2-1),\qquad c_0=2MJ,\\
    &\alpha_\pm=\mp\frac{Ja^2\big[M(E^2+1)\pm(E^2-1)\sqrt{M^2-a^2}\big]}
      {2\sqrt{M^2-a^2}}.
\end{aligned}
    \label{eq:tau-coeffs}
\end{equation}
The two holomorphic integrals are the Abel-map coordinates (arguments of the
Kleinian hyperelliptic $\sigma$), the horizon terms the third-kind
$\ln\sigma$-quotients---the exact genus-2 analogue of the $t$-branch $J_+^t$
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-genus2}]. We built $Y^2=\mathcal S$ in SageMath at $J=-0.9\Jc$
and computed its Riemann period matrix $\tau$; both Riemann relations hold
($\tau=\tau^\top$ to $7\times10^{-12}$, $\operatorname{Im}\tau\succ0$), and
Eq.~\eqref{eq:tau-genus2} reproduces the direct integral to $10^{-15}$. At
$|J|=\Jc$ the factor $f^2$ (from $\Delta-\Jc^2w=f(r^2+c^2)$) makes $r_e$ a
\emph{double} root of $\mathcal S$; the $(r-2M)$ in $\mathcal K_\tau$ cancels one
factor, dropping the radical to the quartic $Q_4$---genus~1, the Weierstrass
separatrix~\eqref{eq:sep-phi}. This double root is also why the retrograde
marginal $J=-\Jc$ reflects with a \emph{corner} (finite $\dd\varphi/\dd r$)
rather than a smooth grazing: only the prograde $+\Jc$, aided by co-rotation,
threads through.

\paragraph{$t$-branch closed form: generic integrand.} The coordinate-time
brachistochrone has $\dd\varphi/\dd r=\mathcal K(r)/\sqrt{\mathcal R(r)}$ with the
\emph{sextic} radical $\mathcal R=r\,Q_2(r)\,[(E^2-1)r+2M]$, genus~2, the quartic
being (no cubic term)
\begin{equation}
    Q_2(r)=E^2r^4+\big[E^2(a^2-J^2)+J^2\big]r^2
      +2M(J-a)\big[E^2(J-a)-2J\big]r+4M^2(J-a)^2,
    \label{eq:t-Q2}
\end{equation}
and a \emph{closed-form} numerator in the problem quantities
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal K(r)=\frac{r\,[(E^2-1)r+2M]\,\big(J(r-2M)+2Ma\big)}{\Delta(r)},
    \label{eq:t-K}
\end{equation}
holding for \emph{any} $(M,a,E,J)$: the third-kind poles sit at the two horizons
$r_\pm$ ($\Delta=0$) and the polynomial part carries the first-/second-kind
pieces (there is \emph{no} pole at $r=0$, cancelled by the leading factor $r$).
By the conformal transfer $n^2_{\rm TK}=n^2_{\rm Kerr}(E_{\rm eff})$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:thakurta}), Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-K} is also the Thakurta--Kerr
$t$-integrand under $E\to E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$, $J\to J/A$, so the conformal
factor is \emph{not} a separate variable of $\varphi(r)$.

\paragraph{$t$-branch separatrix in Weierstrass functions.} At the saddle-node
$J_c^-$ [Eq.~\eqref{eq:Jc-minus}] the radical acquires a
\emph{double root} at the marginal-capture radius $r_*$,
$\mathcal R=(r-r_*)^2 Q_4(r)$, and
$\dd\varphi/\dd r=\mathcal G(r)/\sqrt{Q_4}$ becomes a third-kind elliptic
differential on the quartic $Q_4$ (which retains the root $r=0$). With the same
uniformization $z(r)=\wp^{-1}(A/r+B;g_2,g_3)$ (invariants of $Q_4$) the closed
form is
\begin{equation}
    \varphi(r)=\Lambda_0\,z(r)+\sum_{k}\lambda_k\,
      \ln\frac{\sigma(z-v_k)}{\sigma(z+v_k)},
    \label{eq:t-sep-phi}
\end{equation}
now with third-kind poles only at $c_k\in\{\rp,\rm,r_*\}$---three real ones, with
\emph{no} pole at $r=0$ and no complex pair, unlike the $\tau$-separatrix. Here
$v_k=\wp^{-1}(A/c_k+B)$, $\lambda_k=-A\alpha_k/(c_k^2\wp'(v_k))$, and
$\Lambda_0=\Lambda_{\rm poly}-\sum_k\alpha_k/c_k+\sum_k 2\zeta(v_k)\lambda_k$,
with $\alpha_k$ the residues of $\mathcal G$. For $M=1$, $a=0.9$, $E=1.2$
($r_*=3.514$, $J_c^-=-8.05$) Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-sep-phi} reproduces the Hamiltonian
flow to $2\times10^{-14}$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:t-sep}). So both separatrices are
genus~1 by different mechanisms; only non-separatrix $t$-orbits need genus-2
Kleinian functions.

\paragraph{Separatrix $\Jc$ tracking.} As the control parameter drifts the separatrix
itself moves, $J=\Jc(\lambda)$, so the adiabatic source becomes the \emph{total}
$\lambda$-derivative taken along it, $N\to N_{\rm tot}=N+(\dd\Jc/\dd\lambda)N_J$ with
$N_J=S\,\partial_JK-\tfrac12K\,\partial_JS$, the residue formulae unchanged. The
branches differ only in $\dd\Jc/\dd\lambda$: Vaidya $\dd\Jc/\dd m=\Jc/m$ (scaling
$\Jc=m\,j(E)$); TK $\tau$ $\dd\Jc/\dd E=-E\Jc r_d/DE(r_d)$; TK $t$ two roots $\Jc^\pm$
with $\dd\Jc^\pm/\dd E=-\partial_EQ_2/\partial_JQ_2$ (asymmetric, frame dragging). A
clean consequence closes the logic: at the double root the tracked source vanishes,
$N_{\rm tot}(r_d)=\tfrac12K(r_d)S'(r_d)\,\dd r_d/\dd\lambda=0$ by $S'(r_d)=0$, so the
triple-pole residue drops, $b_3^{\rm track}=0$---tracking softens the singularity
exactly where the double root travels (checked symbolically in Mathematica,
$N_{\rm tot}(r_d)\equiv0\bmod\{S,S'\}$).

\paragraph{Genus-2 Kleinian form and ergosphere penetration.} Away from the
separatrices the sextic $\mathcal R$ keeps six \emph{simple} branch points
(here $\{-4.545,\,-1.722\pm1.793i,\,0,\,1.444,\,2.000\}$), so the curve
$y^2=\mathcal R(r)$ is a genuine genus-2 hyperelliptic Riemann surface and
$\varphi(r)$ closes only in \emph{Kleinian} (hyperelliptic $\sigma$) functions.
With the numerator~\eqref{eq:t-K} the partial-fraction split
$\mathcal K=c_1 r+c_0+\alpha_+/(r-\rp)+\alpha_-/(r-\rm)$ gives
\begin{equation}
    \varphi(r)=\underbrace{c_1\!\!\int\!\frac{r\,\dd r}{\sqrt{\mathcal R}}
      +c_0\!\!\int\!\frac{\dd r}{\sqrt{\mathcal R}}}_{\text{first kind}}
      +\underbrace{\sum_{\pm}\alpha_\pm\!\!\int\!
      \frac{\dd r}{(r-r_\pm)\sqrt{\mathcal R}}}_{\text{third kind}},
    \label{eq:t-genus2}
\end{equation}
the holomorphic pair being the Abel-map coordinates (arguments of the Kleinian
$\sigma$) and the horizon terms the third-kind $\ln\sigma$-quotients---the exact
genus-2 analogue of Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-sep-phi}. For $M=1$, $a=0.9$, $E=1.2$ we
built $y^2=\mathcal R$ in SageMath and computed its Riemann period matrix
$\tau$; both Riemann relations hold ($\tau=\tau^\top$ to $4\times10^{-12}$,
$\operatorname{Im}\tau\succ0$ with eigenvalues $\{0.61,1.08\}$), certifying the
homology basis. The single Abelian integral $\varphi(r)$ does not invert to a
one-$\theta$ quotient $r(\varphi)$ (that needs the 2D Jacobi inversion,
$r=\wp_{22}$ along the trajectory line in the Jacobian, à la
Hackmann--L\"ammerzahl); but Eq.~\eqref{eq:t-genus2} reproduces the Hamiltonian
flow to $2\times10^{-13}$. Physically these genus-2 orbits are exactly the ones
that \emph{penetrate the ergosphere}: the prograde threshold $J_+^t$
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:Jplus-t}] has its turning point \emph{on} $r_e=2M$ (simple root at
$r=2$), while for $J$ just inside the capture window $[J_c^-,J_+^t]$ the turning
point moves \emph{inside} the ergoregion and the closed form stays accurate
across and below $r_e$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:jpt-g2}). This is the \emph{asymmetric}
$t$-window $J\in[J_c^-,J_+^t]=[-8.05,3.43]$; the $\tau$-branch instead penetrates
over the \emph{symmetric} window $|J|<a/E$ (turning exactly on $r_e$ at
$|J|=a/E$), the two-sided asymmetry of the $t$-window being the frame-dragging
wind, absent from the $J^2$-even $\tau$ radial turning. Only the retrograde
$t$-separatrix $J_c^-$ (the saddle-node) never reaches the ergosphere---it
spirals onto $r_*=3.514>r_e$.

\paragraph{Stationary-limit conserved quantities.} At $A=1$ the branch flows admit
the closed first integrals used for validation,
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal K_\tau=J,\qquad
    \mathcal K_t=\frac{fJ+2Ma/r}{E},\qquad
    \mathcal K_\eta=\frac{fJ}{E},
    \label{eq:kerr-conserved}
\end{equation}
the equatorial Kerr doranTau/doranT constants.

\paragraph{Rotational inversion function.} The same-launch $t/\tau$ ordering
inverts where $\rho(r_*)^2=H_{t0}/H_{\tau0}$ with the explicit ratio of
centrifugal potentials
\begin{equation}
    \rho=\frac{A^2}{\Ehat}\Big(f+\frac{2Ma}{rJ}\Big),
    \label{eq:rho-explicit}
\end{equation}
$\rho<1$ giving $t$ deeper, $\rho>1$ giving $\tau$ deeper.

\paragraph{Conformal trichotomy.} With constant $A$ the separatrix momentum is
$\Jc=sA^2/\Ehat$, and above it the smooth periapsis is the outer root of
\begin{equation}
    \Delta(r)-\Big(\tfrac{J}{A}\Big)^2\Big[\big(\tfrac{\Ehat}{A}\big)^2-f(r)\Big]=0,
    \label{eq:periapsis-root}
\end{equation}
while $|J|<\Jc$ reflects at a cusp $r_{\min}=\re$ (as does the retrograde
marginal $-\Jc$, forced counter-rotation forbidding it inside $\re$), and only
the prograde $+\Jc$ crosses to $\rp$.

\paragraph{Closed toroidal averages.} The secular source $\langle S_1\rangle$ of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:S1} follows from the orbit-plane averages ($\cos\theta=\sin i\,
\sin\psi$, $k=1-J^2/L^2$)
\begin{equation}
    \Big\langle\cos^2\theta\Big\rangle=\tfrac{k}{2},\quad
    \Big\langle\tfrac{1}{\sin^2\theta}\Big\rangle=\tfrac{L}{|J|},\quad
    \big\langle p_\theta^2\big\rangle=L(L-|J|),
    \label{eq:averages}
\end{equation}
with $\langle\cos2\theta\,p_\theta^2\rangle=|J|(L-|J|)$.

\subsection{Reproducing the reductions}
\label{app:reproduce}
Every coefficient in the adiabatic corrections is fixed by a \emph{single}
polynomial identity the reader can solve directly; nothing is fitted. The depth-one
reduction $\partial_\lambda\varphi_0=\mathcal{A}/\sqrt S+\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k U_k$
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:dEphi0}; likewise the Vaidya mass reduction of
Sec.~\ref{sec:vaidya-adiabatic}], differentiated in $r$ and cleared of the radical
via $\partial_\lambda F=N_\lambda/S^{3/2}$, is equivalent to
\begin{equation}
    2\,\mathcal{A}'S-\mathcal{A}\,S'+2S\sum_{k=0}^4 c_k r^k=2N_\lambda,
    \qquad \mathcal{A}=\sum_{i=0}^5 a_i r^i,
    \label{eq:reduction-identity}
\end{equation}

gives a nonsingular $11\times11$ linear system in $\{a_0,\dots,a_5,c_0,\dots,c_4\}$;
one linear solve returns the $c_k$ rationally in the metric parameters ($S$ the
branch radical: $S$ on the $\tau$-branch, $R_6=r\,Q_2\,DE$ on the $t$-branch). Here

cancels), $N_\lambda=R_6\partial_E\mathcal K-\tfrac12\mathcal K\partial_E R_6$ for

\section{Complete first-order extended-Hamiltonian correction}
\label{app:firstorder}
This appendix derives Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}---the complete first-order (on-shell
\emph{plus} off-shell) adiabatic correction---from the extended Hamiltonian and
specializes it to the $t$-, $\tau$- and Vaidya branches, so a reader can reconstruct
it without the code.

\paragraph{Notation and the slow datum.}
$\Ehat$ and $J\equiv J_{\rm TK}=p_\varphi$ are the \emph{conserved} control charges;
the effective unit-mass Kerr charges on the frozen slice are $E_{\rm eff}=\Ehat/A$ and
$J_{\rm eff}=J/A$, both of conformal weight $A^{-1}$. The slow datum is $s$ ($s=\eta$
Thakurta--Kerr, $s=v$ Vaidya); $A(s)=A_0(1+\varepsilon s+\cdots)$ with $A_0\equiv1$ and
\emph{dimensionless} rate $\varepsilon=M A_{,\eta}/A$ (Vaidya $\varepsilon=M\dot m/m$).
The Euler operator generating the drift of the frozen family is
$\Theta\equiv E_{\rm eff}\partial_{E_{\rm eff}}+J_{\rm eff}\partial_{J_{\rm eff}}$
(Thakurta--Kerr), $\Theta\equiv m\partial_m$ (Vaidya), since $\dd E_{\rm eff}/\dd
s=-\varepsilon E_{\rm eff}$ etc.\ ($\dd m/\dd s=+\varepsilon m$).

\paragraph{Extended system and first order.}
Adjoin $s$ with conjugate $p_s$: $\mathcal H=p_s+H_0(r,p_r;E_{\rm eff}(s),J_{\rm
eff}(s))=0$. Free-arrival transversality sets $H_0=0$ ($p_s=0$) at the endpoint
$\lambda=0$. Along the flow $\dd p_s/\dd\lambda=\varepsilon\,\Theta H_0$, so
$p_s^{(1)}(\lambda)\equiv S(\lambda)=\int_0^\lambda\Theta H_0\,\dd\lambda'$,
\emph{terminally anchored} ($S=0$ at $\lambda=0$). Because $H_0=-\varepsilon S$ the
orbit leaves the frozen shell at $O(\varepsilon)$; linearizing $H_0$ at fixed $r$ gives
$\delta p_r=(\lambda\,\Theta H_0-S)/H_{0,p_r}$, the axial costate remaining conserved
($\delta J=0$; it is $J_{\rm eff}=J/A$ that breathes). Integrating $\dd\varphi/\dd
r=G_0+\varepsilon[\partial_{p_r}G_0\,\delta p_r-\lambda\,\Theta G_0]+O(\varepsilon^2)$
reproduces Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact}. Here $G_0=H_{0,J}/H_{0,p_r}$ is the frozen shape
integrand and $\dd\lambda/\dd r=1/H_{0,p_r}$ the clock density, both on the ingoing
root $p_{r0}(r)$ ($H_{0,p_r}<0$). The reduction holds on compact sub-arcs bounded away
from the turning point ($H_{0,p_r}\to0$).

\paragraph{Branch specialization.}
The frozen $H_0$ per branch: $H_\eta,H_t,H_\tau$ (Thakurta--Kerr,
Eqs.~\eqref{eq:Heta}) and, for Vaidya, $H_v$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:Hv} with
\begin{equation}
    \Theta H_v=m\,\partial_m H_v=m\!\left[-\frac{2p_r}{r}
    +\frac{\sqrt{\Ehat^2 p_r^2+J^2/r^2}}{r\sqrt w}\right],\quad w=\Ehat^2-f.
\end{equation}
Since $m$ \emph{increases} while $E_{\rm eff},J_{\rm eff}$ decrease, the Vaidya
correction is Eq.~\eqref{eq:adiab-exact} with $(\lambda\Theta H_0-S)\to(S-\lambda\Theta
H_0)$ and $-\lambda\Theta G_0\to+\lambda\Theta G_0$; the proper-time branch uses
$H_\tau$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:Htau-vaidya}. All four cases close the correction to
$O(\varepsilon^2)$: least-squares log--log slopes $1.95\pm0.01$ ($t$), $1.97\pm0.02$
($\tau$) and $2.16\pm0.11$ (a second parameter set), Fig.~\ref{fig:phi-true-dynamic},
and $2.00$ (Vaidya, Fig.~\ref{fig:vaidya-offshell}), versus $\approx1$ for the on-shell
term alone (\texttt{ThakurtaMetric/adiabatic\_first\_order\_exact.py},
\texttt{VaidyaMetric/vaidya\_first\_order\_offshell.py}). The explicit assembly of the
off-shell term---itself a length-two iterated Abelian integral, since $S$ is already an
integral---into the special-function basis of App.~\ref{app:explicit} is supplied in
the reproducibility package.
